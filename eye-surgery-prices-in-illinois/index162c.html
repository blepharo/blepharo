Eye Surgery Prices In Illinois

Eyelids is a unique component of the face around the eyes, carries out not only a number of practical functions, but also plays a huge aesthetic role.

Eyelid surgery, or blepharoplasty in Illinois is an operation designed to change eye shape, the primary purpose of which is to achieve the desired aesthetic effect.

During surgery is performed excision of the skin and remove excess fat deposits.

Indications for eyelid surgery are:

– the overhang of weakened skin upper eyelid that violates contour eyes;
– excess fatty tissue of the upper or lower eyelid, causing puffiness;
– “Heavy” eyelids;
– sagging of the lower eyelid.

Illinois blepharoplasty can get rid of excess skin and fat herniation, eliminate defects and reduce the visibility of age-related changes, change the shape of the eyes and eyelids.

Types of blepharoplasty technique.

There Are the following types the eyelid surgery:

1. Circular blepharoplasty is aimed at correcting the upper and lower eyelids.
2. The upper blepharoplasty is an operation on the upper eyelids; performed through an incision in the natural crease of the eyelid, allows to remove excess fat and excess skin and lifting the tissue and muscles eyelids.
3. Lower blepharoplasty is performed on the lower eyelids, excision and removal of fat tissue is through the incision on the inside of the eyelid (transconjunctival blepharoplasty) or on the outside, directly underneath lash line.
4. Asian blepharoplasty is aimed at correction of the upper eyelid and smoothing of epicanthus and change eye shape.

In addition, blepharoplasty can be performed in combination with other procedures (an endoscopy forehead, circular face lift, etc.). The eyelid procedure is performed under general or local anesthesia and lasts for 1.5 to 3 hours.

The recovery period and recommendations.

Primary recovery after eyelid surgery in Illinois takes from one to two weeks. Patient’s stay in hospital last one day if the surgery to lift eyelids was been performed under anesthesia. During the first 3-4 days after eyelid surgery should use antiseptic eyedrops.

After this time, sutures are removed. Swelling and bruising disappear within 1.5-2 weeks. The sutures are absorbed within three to four weeks. In this period of time should be limited sun exposure and is not recommended to use the sauna or solarium.

Full recovery occurs within 1.5-2 months. You can also read the blog of our patients, who described the nuances of blepharoplasty, read the comments anesthesiologist and a plastic surgeon who performed the cosmetic surgery eyelids.

Contraindications.

Among the contraindications to blepharoplasty: diabetes mellitus, exacerbation of chronic disease, conjunctivitis, blepharospasm, blood diseases, violation of its coagulation, acute and chronic eye diseases, sexually transmitted diseases.

The results of blepharoplasty and possible complications.

The result of the eyelid surgery can be assessed no earlier than 3 – 6 weeks. During this time, the flexibility of the tissues is restored in the eyelids area, scars are absorbed and swelling and bruises completely disappear. Typically, results of blepharoplasty are more durable in comparison with the results of other plastic surgeries.

Blepharoplasty in Illinois enables to correct of weakened skin of the eyelids, eliminate fat herniation, and thus save the patient from a tired and old-looking, “rejuvenate” it. Blepharoplasty allows almost fully and permanently remove the bags under the eyes. Typically, a second operation may be required no earlier than 12 years.

The risk complications of blepharoplasty.

As with other surgical procedures, there is risk of some complications. It can be as specific complications, and complications associated with anesthesia in the form of allergy to local anesthetics. Non-specific complications of blepharoplasty such as:

  • Bleeding and hematoma formation. This complication can occur at any time after surgery. It is recommended to stop taking certain drugs before surgery in order to avoid it.
  • In some cases there may be a disruption seams.
  • Infectious-inflammatory complications. Antibiotics are used to prevent them.
  • The formation of rough postoperative scar. This is rare. Most often is associated with concomitant hematomas and infectious complications.
  • Violation of lacrimation, which is manifested by dryness of eyes.
