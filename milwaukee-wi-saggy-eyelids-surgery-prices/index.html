How is blepharoplasty performed?

Before the bottom eyelid surgery, the surgeon applies the marking, which further helps him to make incisions to remove adipose tissue, as well as stitches. The incisions are made on the natural curve of the eyes and under the eyelashes of the lower eyelids, so that the sutures subsequently remain invisible.

Among age-related patients, there are cases when it is required to strengthen the fixation of weakened orbicular muscle of eye. This is due to the fixation of a part of the muscle fibers to the periosteum of the orbit, this manipulation is called cantopexy.

The eye lid operation is performed under local or intravenous anesthesia, depending on the specialist’s decision. The duration of the eyelid surgery is not more than an hour.

What Is Blepharoplasty, Eye Lift Or Eye Tuck

What is a blepharoplasty?

Blepharoplasty is an operation to change the shape of the eyelids and eyes. If the cosmetic eye lift is performed on the upper eyelids, it usually consists of removing excess skin, sometimes fatty tissue.

The seam after this operation is invisible, because it remains hidden in the natural skin fold of the eyelid. When carrying out blepharoplasty on the lower eyelids, usually remove excess fatty tissue.

Usually this is achieved by truncating or more even distribution of adipose tissue. In this case, transconjunctival surgery is performed, which does not imply the presence of scars. In some cases, correction of the skin of the lower eyelids is necessary. This can be done through surgery, or using CO2 laser or chemical peeling.
