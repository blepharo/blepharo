Preparing for blepharoplasty.

During the planning of eyelid surgery requires careful examination of the patient and the choice of surgical technique. Blepharoplasty of upper and lower eyelids did not initially suggests a well-defined set of actions.

Also, before blepharoplasty the patient is required to do the necessary tests and undergo a medical examination: to determine visual acuity, the function of the eye muscles, to evaluate the function of the optic nerve, to determine the tone of the eyelids.

A week before the blepharoplasty you should give up smoking and alcohol. Smoking decreases intensity of blood circulation and thus slows down the healing of wounds.

Possible complications and side effects.

Any surgery involves the presence of complications and Tucson blepharoplasty is no exception. Here are some of them:

  • Swelling;
  • Dry eye;
  • Bruises;
  • Light sensitivity, temporarily disturbed eyesight;
  • Scarring at the site of skin incisions;
  • The development of the ptosis of the eyelid (blepharoptosis);
  • Ectropion due to excessive tension of the skin;
  • Violation symmetry of the eyes.

Note that the swelling, bruising and dryness of the eyes disappear with the restoration of the patient’s appearance. Other postoperative complications are minimal and much less common.

The upper blepharoplasty can be performed several times, but only after 7 or 10 years after the first operation. The lower blepharoplasty is possible in case of a medical indication.
