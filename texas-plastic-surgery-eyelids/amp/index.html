Texas Plastic Surgery Eyelids

Blepharoplasty (eyelid surgery) in Texas is currently one of the most frequently performed aesthetic procedures, but is still considered a technically complex operation.

With age, because of excess of skin and fat, “bags” and “hernias” are formed in the lower eyelids, visually aging the face.

These aesthetic problems are solved with the help of blepharoplasty, which magically rejuvenates the whole face.

The surgeon’s goal is to eliminate excess skin and fat, tighten the auxiliary structures of the eye and at the same time ensure a natural result.

Transcutaneous lower lid blepharoplasty (classical operation on the lower eyelids) in TX provides an external incision of the skin followed by a cosmetic intradermal suture. With this plastic surgery can remove significant excess skin and fat in the periorbital area, eliminate expressed nasolacrimal furrow at almost any age.

Transconjunctival blepharoplasty of lower eyelids is a plastic operation on the eyelids, in which the internal incision is performed in the region of the conjunctiva, that is, leaves no visible scars and scarring.

This operation is indicated to patients up to 40-45 years old, who do not yet have significant age-related changes, that is, the skin and other tissues are not strongly stretched, and the fatty deposits are insignificant.

This surgery is also offered to elderly patients if the excess skin is moderate. This surgical technique gives fewer complications, a more natural appearance and a faster recovery (5 days instead of 7-10 after transcutaneous blepharoplasty).

Before the eyelid surgery in TX you need to discuss many questions with a plastic surgeon: first of all, tell about your expectations from the eye tuck surgery, as well as the presence of chronic diseases, including hypertension, angina pectoris, diabetes, liver diseases, thyroid gland, a blood coagulation.

Asian Blepharoplasty With Doctor Anand G. Shah

A candidate for blepharoplasty should tell his “eye” history of lachrymation, dry eyes, frequent blinking, redness and burning eyes, other problems, as well as the use of contact lenses, ophthalmic surgery, for example LASIK Eye Surgery (after which preliminary consultation and permission of the ophthalmologist is needed).

Patients, wearing contact lenses are a special group of candidates for surgery, since eyelid surgery can affect the curvature of the cornea of the eye, making the use of contact lenses uncomfortable or even dangerous.

Patients should stop using contact lenses one week before surgery and within a few weeks after surgery to ensure the healing of the eyelids without manipulation during their use.

Should also be excluded smoking, drinking alcohol, taking medications, including supplements (taking aspirin and other anticoagulants should be stopped two weeks before the eye tuck surgery and within one week after it, to prevent bleeding).

Blepharoplasty of lower eyelids in Texas is not intended to eliminate all wrinkles in the circumorbital area.

This problem can be solved by other procedures – chemical peeling, laser or mechanical skin resurfacing.

In addition, the use of autologous fat injections or hyaluronic fillers to restore volume a of the periorbital eye area in more complex cases that are not corrected only with a blepharoplasty provided the best result.

TX blepharoplasty is an operation aimed at improving the appearance of the eyelids and the periorbital area (around the eyes), by removing excess skin, partially removing or redistributing intraorbital fat, and tightening the circular eye muscle.

The eyelids and the periorbital region are most prone to age-related changes. Very tender skin of the eyelids faster than any other gives age. Changes affect not only the skin, but also deep structures.

External manifestations are: excess skin in the upper eyelid, which can overhang and even reduce the field of vision, wrinkles and so-called “bags” under the eyes appear in the lower eyelids area.

The situation in such cases can be improved with an operation called blepharoplasty. Aesthetic blepharoplasty is one of the most popular aesthetic operations. There is no age limit for blepharoplasty in Texas. This operation can be carried out both in young and old age. The greatest number of operations is performed after 35 years old.

The skin of the eyelids is the thinnest on the surface of the human body, it gives the ability for better and faster healing, compared to other parts of the body.

In most cases, the scar passes along the natural fold of the eyelid, and it is almost impossible to discern after the eyelid correction surgery.

The indications for blepharoplasty of the upper eyelids are:
  1. Excess skin and, in some cases, the muscles of the upper eyelid, which leads to the overhang of the upper eyelid, which sometimes reaches even the lash line;
  2. Fatty hernia of the eyelids, which accentuate the overhanging of excess skin and smooth out the vault of the upper eyelid.
