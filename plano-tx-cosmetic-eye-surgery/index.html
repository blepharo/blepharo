New approaches to lower eyelid blepharoplasty

Our understanding of the youthful appearance of the lower eyelids has certainly changed over the past 10 years.

An old style blepharoplasty generally consisted of a subcillary incision (just below the lash line), raising of a skin muscle flap and removal of bulging fat and excision of redundant skin of the lower eyelid

This method, on occasion lead to a hollowing out of the lower lids and sometimes a pulling down of the lower eyelid. This either lead to lower eyelid malposition or a pulling down of the lateral aspect of the eyelid

We rarely perform this type of reduction blepharoplasty on the lower eyelids. Today we take an entirely new approach to lower eyelid rejuvenation. The primary emphasis is on restoring youthful fullness. The bulging fat of the lower eyelids is used to fill in the tear trough zone and to blend the junction between the lower eyelid and the cheek
