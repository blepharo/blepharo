Eyelid Surgery, Steri-Strip and Ointment

You are asking good questions, but you won’t get a good answer until you ask you surgeon. The particulars of your surgery are unknown, so I cannot give you good advice. In general, Steri-Strips are used to secure the sutures used to close the eyelid incision

Dr Philip Solomon Facial Rejuvenation

These are placed on the forehead between the eye brows and to the outside of the eyes. They can also be used on the lower eyelid to support the lid after surgery. Removal should be performed only under your surgeon’s direction, so that the closures remain closed

Normally ointment is not placed on the Steri-Strips, as this may lead to premature loosening. Give your doctor a call. They will appreciate you taking an active role in your care and your desire to heal quickly

You generally get what you pay for.

One of the reasons you may have found a difference in the cost of a Blepharoplasty among different physicians is that there is a difference in Blepharoplasties among different physicians and for different reasons. In its simplest terms, a Blepharoplasty removes excess tissue from the eye

Dr Solomon Before And After

Within that general definition, you can stabilize the canthus, stabilize the entire lid, remove the corrugator muscles to reduce the wrinkles between the eyes, elevate the brow, reposition or remove fat, detach the arcus (the lower attachment of the muscle, etc

in addition to removing excess skin (which almost all share). It is the combination of all these things that allows us to create a truly cosmetic result with minimal risk of complications. Simply removing skin, muscle and fat, which a lot of physicians still do, is fast and easy and less expensive, but does not give one the advantages of the newer methods, which take more time and effort, thus raising the cost

Also, since there are 2 codes for each eyelid, one for reconstructive, many people will do a very simple procedure to fix the immediate problem if the procedure is being paid by insurance, since that is all the insurance companies are willing to pay for
Essentially, you generally get what you pay for.

DO NOT BOARD A PLANE AFTER EYELID SURGERY

This is one of the biggest mistakes that can cost you your vision. The changes in air pressure in the cabin can open up a blood vessel that was cauterized during surgery and lead to a hematoma (collection of blood under skin) that starts pressing on your optic (eye) nerve and causes BLINDNESS

For upper eyes without much fat removal the earliest you can fly is one week. For lower eyes three weeks
