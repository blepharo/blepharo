Healing time after eyelid surgery

Though it is difficult to assess without a proper exam in the office, it appears that your eyelid incisions are healing normally. I specialize in eyelid surgery and it is common to have asymmetric swelling, bulges, bruising, and bumpy scars for the first few weeks after surgery.

If you experience the same issue a few weeks after surgery, I would discuss with your surgeon gentle massage, and possibly a steroid cream, but you should see improvement over time, which can take several weeks to months.

Undoing a double fold eyelid is very risky

The fold is created by adhesions between the eyelid skin and the tendon that raises and lowers the upper eyelid. This attachment may exist in an asian eyelid and creates a double fold or may be absent accounting for a single fold.

Typically in Asian eyelid surgery (and yes there is such a thing as asian eyelid surgery), looks to create a double fold. Undoing a double fold is likely to result in a very unacceptable outcome.

Similarly, trying to lower a double fold is also so difficult that many believe that it can’t really be done. My recommendation to you would be to have a tiny bit of skin removed on the right side so that you have a uniform but low fold on the right side and have the left side done to exactly match the right side.

When this is done, you should also have slight traction from the top of the eyelid platform to help rotate the eyelashes upward. This procedure is known as an anchor blepharoplasty and needs to be done while you are awake.

Blepharoplasty without epinephrine

Considering you are very sensitive to epinephrine, the concentration of it can be reduced for your surgery. I personally perform most lower blepharoplasties in the office using just local anesthetic. I only use 5-6 ml of the solution for the procedure.

That’s not much epinephrine. I could dilute it by half for someone that is sensitive like you and it would be highly unlikely to cause any symptoms at all. Theoretically you could do the surgery without the epinephrine, but it would easier for the surgeon if a little could be used.

Hematoma after upper eyelid surgery

The risk for hematoma formation after elective upper eyelid surgery is greatest within the first 24 hours after surgery. After that it is extremely unusual but might occur if you are using any anticoagulants including aspirin or NSAID’s

Steri Strips on Eyelid Incisions

Surgeons have different techniques for closing wounds to minimize scar. Some like steri strips over wounds. I personally do not like them on the eyelid, since the eyelid is a dynamic structure and the ‘stiff’ strips can be uncomfortable as you have noticed

I would consult with your surgeon as to whether ointment is perfered over the steri strips. In my experience it is not a problem.
