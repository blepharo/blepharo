Blepharoplasty cost

Cosmetic surgery is not a commodity. Techniques, experience, artistic sense, and therefore results (what you get for your money) vary tremendously from one surgeon to the next – and as such, the cost varies, and should vary, from one surgeon to the next. You can find surgeons doing eyelids for $1000 and some doing the same surgery for $6000. This doesn’t mean that you necessarily get what you pay for since some surgeons are very good at marketing themselves to appear like expert surgeons (one way to do this is simply to charge more) when they are not.

Ultimately, the best way to determine whether the price a surgeon is charging is worth the results, is to go to their Web site and look at his/her “before and after” photos.

There should be a LOT of them if the surgeon has the desired experience. Then check to see if the results are CONSISTENTLY excellent, especially look for eyelids that have a similar appearance to yours.

Surgeons with few photos and/or photos demonstrating minimal, inadequate, or undesirable changes obviously should not be charging as much. (Richard Parfitt, MD, Madison Facial Plastic Surgeon)

Cost of eyelid surgery in Albany NY

As mentioned by others, what affects the total cost most is whether you are correcting both sets of lids (upper & lower) or one set of lids. At my practice in Albany NY, a standard 4 lid blepharoplasty with skin and fat excision and/or repositioning usually costs in the neighborhood of $6,000.

This price includes all fees (anesthesia, hospital/surgery-center, surgeon fees, etc.). An upper lid blepharoplasty alone (all fees included) is usually under $3,000 while a lower alone (with anesthesia) is usually around $3,500 (because of the fat excisioning and/or repositioning). So you save a bit by doing both together.

The other factor impacting cost is the type of anesthesia needed to safely perform your procedure. Cases that require general anesthesia (e.g. 4 lid blepharoplasty, lower lid cases which involve fat removal and/or repositioning, etc.) will obviously cost more than those requiring only local anesthesia (e.g. some upper lid lifts where only skin is being excised) since general requires an anesthesiologist.

That said, given the delicate nature and complexity of the procedure, do make sure that your surgeon is board certified and has a great deal of experience with facial cosmetic surgery, particularly eyelids. (William F. DeLuca Jr, MD, Albany Plastic Surgeon)
