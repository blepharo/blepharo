Blepharopalsty complications

Here are the more common and more remote complications and risks of a blepharoplasty in the upper and lower eyelid from more common to least common:

1. Assymetry: removing the skin or fat on both sides is an art not a science

Sometimes there can be residual assymetry that occurs. A little asymmetry actually can look a little more natural than if it is “perfect”. This can be corrected witha touch up procedure if more skin or fat needs to be removed

2. Irregular incision: The incision of a blepharoplasty in the upper eyelid should be in the lid crease. In the lower eyelid it should be near the lash line. Deviation from that can create a more noticeable scar

This should soften in appearance in time but is possible. This is determined by the surgeon as they are doing the baggy eye surgery. The irregular incision can be fixed sometimes with another touch up procedure.

3. Bleeding: There is always a risk of bleeding after eyelid surgery

After blepharoplasty if a bleed occurs behind the eye, increased pressure can damage the optic nerve that supplies the eye and cause vision loss. This is an uncommon occurrence but can happen. After surgery for eyelids i tell patients to take it easy, no heavy bending lift stooping for 10 days

5. Lower eyelid retraction: If too much skin is removed in the lower eyelid, the lower lid can be pulled down. Steroid injections can occasionally help this but further surgery is usually neeeded.

6. Slow healing: sometimes it can take a longer for complete healing to occur in some patients. This is dependant on constituion and 99% of people tend to heal up perfectly over time..just takes longer.
