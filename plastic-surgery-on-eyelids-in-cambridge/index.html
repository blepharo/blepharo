Eyelid surgery or blepharoplasty in Cambridge is a procedure that addresses the excess skin and fatty tissue around the eyes that worsens with age and causes a weighing down of the lids and sagging and puffiness. This make you look tired or less alert.

Removing the excess skin, both of the upper and lower eyelids can open up the eyes, take away the tired heavy look, and make you appear fresher and more alert and younger. This surgery is typically done as an outpatient (so you can go home that same day) and with a general anesthetic (so that you are all the way asleep).

You will need to keep ice on your eyes off and on for the first 24 hours and have the head of your bed elevated or sleep in a recliner chair for at least 10 to 14 days to reduce the swelling. Your surgeon will see you back the next morning to check for any problems and then at about one week to remove stitches. After that, you will be seen periodically to make sure that there are no problems with healing.

You will need to avoid any heavy lifting or straining or vigorous exercise for approximately three weeks. If you have a sedentary or light job you should be able to go back to full duty after approximately one week. If your job is more strenuous then you may need to be on light duty until about three weeks. Most of the swelling goes down in the first couple of weeks and the eyelid scars usually heal quite quickly and are hard to find once they have matured.

Also, once the stitches are out you can resume wearing makeup and camouflage any bruising or early redness of the scars. It is best to discuss the details of your situation at the time of your initial consultation with your surgeon. Then you can to into the surgery with a specific plan in mind to make your experience as smooth and rewarding as possible. (Marie E. Montag, MD, Omaha Plastic Surgeon)
