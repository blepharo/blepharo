Skin pinch a good alternative for some

The skin pinch technique for lower eyelid blepharoplasty is a modification of a more traditional technique. It has a lower risk for complications since it only removes a small amount of skin from the lower eyelid

It will not, however, effect the extra fat many patient have effecting their lower eyelids. This is often the dominant problem for many patients.

Sometimes, the skin pinch can be combined with a transconjunctival blepharoplasty to allow for removal and recontouring or extra fat and removal of a small amount of extra skin without disrupting the muscle layers that are important in providing support to the lower eyelid

In the situation of dry eyes, it might be marginally advantageous than some other techniques. There is no one technique that is best for all patients. Your plastic surgeon should consider all factors when making a recommendation about how best to proceed

Lower Eye Lid “Pinch” Blepharoplasty

Pinch blepharoplasty is commonly performed to remove excess lower eye lid skin only. It does minimize the risks of eye lid surgery as it is a “skin only” procedure. Your surgeon must make sure to take only enough skin so as to not distort the lower eye lid and cause an ectropion.

Local anesthesia works for upper blepharoplasty

Local anesthesia is a safe and effective way to do an upper blepharoplasty. There is very little concern about injuring the eyes as they are closed. If you need to calm your nerves you can take an oral sedative or the procedure can be done with iv sedation.

All of these techniques are safe. If your surgeon experience with this technique then you should do well.
