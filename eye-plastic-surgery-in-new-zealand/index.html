When is blepharoplasty (eyelid lift) covered by insurance?

Whenever we deal with insurance companies, the answer is complicated. In short, it’s best to check with your insurance company, or have your doctor’s office check for you. But these are the general requirements: Most insurance companies will cover blepharoplasty if all 3 requirements are met

  1. Documented photo of eyelid droop or dermatochlasia (excess skin above the eyelid)
  2. Documented clinical exam by two physicians: plastic surgeon and ophthalmologist.
  3. Improvement in superior visual field test with taped eyelids compared to untaped (this is done by the eye doctor)

(Basically you have to see better to make it a non cosmetic case)

Insurance companies never cover the lower eyelid blepharoplasty, but many patients choose to do this at the same time. This is done with a separate cosmetic fee

 

Medical insurance and blepharoplasty

If you feel that your eyelid skin is truly impairing your health or medical well being, then you may be a true candidate for a blepharoplasty. If it is for cosmetic gain, it probably isn’t covered

The only way that insurance will cover an upper eyelid lift is if it can be demonstrated that your vision is compromised. This requires formal evaluation by an ophthalomologist and the test is called a “visual field test
