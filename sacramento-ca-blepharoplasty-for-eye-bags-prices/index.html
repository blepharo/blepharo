How is blepharoplasty performed?

Doctor Thomas E. Kaniff Blepharoplasty Surgery 3 Days After

Blepharoplasty is performed both under local and under general anesthesia, the method of performing the eye surgery and the amount of surgical intervention is determined by our specialist taking into account the condition of the upper and lower eyelids of the patient and your wishes.

Depending on the volume of the eye surgery, the time it takes to perform it varies within three hours. After the eye tuck surgery, the patient is unable to work for several days, usually not more than a week.

What are possible problems may occur during eyelid surgery?

Problems do not arise with the skilled planning of the eye tuck surgery and with its professional performing. Of course, the first days after surgery, there may be a swelling of the eyelids, some difficulties in closing the eyes.

But swelling soon disappears, and with it all the unpleasant sensations. Of course, it is necessary to comply with all the recommendations of the surgeon for care of the eyelids in the postoperative period.

Will I Have Visible Scars from Eyelid Surgery?

No, they will not visible. Your eyes will look completely natural, the look will again become young, open and clear.

What tests should be performed before the eye tuck surgery?

Like before any other surgical operation, you need to perform a number of tests:

  1. general blood analysis,
  2. general urine analysis,
  3. A blood test for clotting,
  4. A blood test for syphilis,
  5. Blood test for HIV,
  6. A blood test for hepatitis,
  7. ECG.

If the patient has chronic diseases or if the above tests do not eliminate doubts of the doctor, he can prescribe additional examinations.
