Hematoma after eye cosmetic surgery.

Hematoma is a collection of blood under the skin. I have not seen one in any upper blepharoplasty in my 28 years. However, it still is a risk that everyone should be aware. Some factors that increase the risk are use of aspirin or anti-inflammatory meds before eye cosmetic surgery, too much activity while one heals (bending over, stooping over, exercise) or use of anticoagulants

I tell my patients that the risk is greatest in the first week but also tell them to avoid those risk factors for two weeks.
