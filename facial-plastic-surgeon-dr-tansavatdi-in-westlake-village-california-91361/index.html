Am I a candidate for a lip lift?
Lip lift
Jul 8, 2020
A (subnasal) lip lift raises the center of your upper lip but will not address the corners. From the pictures, it looks like you have had fillers before. The corners are down-turned and can be somewhat improved with Botox to the depressor muscles of your lower lip. A corner lip lift could add more benefit since your upper lip hangs over the corner of the lips. 
Am I a good candidate for a lip lift to balance my facial features?
Lip lift and rhinoplasty
Jun 9, 2020
Based on the shape of your nose and the droop of your tip on side view, a lip lift will decrease the distance between your nose and the upper lip border which could possibly increase the prominence of the droop of the nasal tip. I would recommend both a tip rhinoplasty to raise and elevate the nasal tip and combine this with a lip lift for the best outcome and balance of your facial features.

I'm doing a facelift and Sculptra, which is better to do first?
Facelift combined with Sculptra
Sep 28, 2019
The best time to replace volume loss is after the facelift since a facelift will reposition volume in the face and add volume in areas that were previously depleted. However, certain areas can be volumized at the same time as a facelift such as areas of hollowing in the temple and around the eyes with a fat transfer. Other areas like the cheeks that are lifted in a facelift should probably be addressed after the surgery at which time you can choose fat grafting or Sculptra injections.
