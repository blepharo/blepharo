Blepharoplasty surgery – How It Works

Blepharoplasty in Buffalo works primarily in the upper eyelids to remove redundant skin and re-shape the eyelid to make it look fuller.

The lower eyelid is to remove potentially crepey skin, bulging and fullness, and to blend the eye to cheek junction. It is one of the more complicated finesse operations in aesthetic surgery.

Therefore, one should choose a plastic surgeon that has expertise in both primary and secondary blepharoplasty to optimize your result.

Often, one will need to have lifting of the outer canthal area to shape the eyelid to make it look more youthful as well. (Rod J. Rohrich, MD, Dallas Plastic Surgeon)

The eyelid has two components, the upper lid and the lower lid. All four lids can be corrected or just one pair.

The procedure can consist of removal of the fat pockets, two pockets in the upper lid, three in the lower, and removal of excess skin, as needed Some patients have it done for cosmetic reasons such as “big bags under the eyes”, or в “puffy upper lids”.

It can also used to help the peripheral vision if the upper lids are drooping too low covering the eye and blocking vision.

Both upper and lower eyelid surgeries can be done at the same time, under general or local anesthesia, as an out-patient procedure.

Buffalo Blepharoplasty surgery is considered surgical rejuvenation of the eyes. (Yael Halaas, MD, Manhattan Facial Plastic Surgeon)

Blepharoplasty surgery to see and look better

Blepharoplasty surgery in Buffalo is the medical term for eyelid surgery. There are upper eyelids and lower eyelids. Each set of eyelids have different surgeries and different results. Basically upper eyelid surgery is to remove excess skin which causes droopiness of the upper eyelids. Patients often notice that they can actually see better and everything appears brighter after having upper eyelid surgery. Lower eyelid surgery is usually done to remove bagginess due to excess skin and fat.

After eyelid surgery, patient usually not only look younger, but more rested and refreshed. The surgery is done as an outpatient, under local anesthesia with IV sedation. Stitches are usually removed in three to five days. Bruising usually is minimal. Asian blepharoplasty is upper eyelid surgery on Asians to create a fold in the upper eyelid that is normally not present. It is done by removing skin and fat, and putting in some internal sutures to create the fold. Normally it is done under local anesthesia and the external stitches are removed in three to five days. (James Tang, MD, Houston Plastic Surgeon)

Eyelid Surgery To Achieve Youthful Look – Blepharoplasty Surgery

Facial aging affects the entire face, but nowhere is it more noticeable than in the area around the eyes. The eyes are truly the window to our souls and tell everyone how we are feeling.

Aging of the soft tissue in this area, not only makes us look older, but can change how we are perceived. Excess eyelid skin can make us look tired and less energetic, when associated with brow descent it can even make us look angry. For these reasons, blepharoplasty is one of the most commonly requested cosmetic surgery procedures. When aging occurs, patients develop excess eyelid skin with prominent fat pads. This is typically one of the earliest signs of facial aging and with time it becomes progressively more noticeable. This problem is most appropriately treated with blepharoplasty surgery.
