Who Is the Best Surgeon?

There are a lot of terrific Surgeons in your area, so I recommend that you seek consultations with at least a few. Luckily for you, the trend in facial surgery is to achieve a refreshed natural look

As far as what specialty would provide you the best opportunity for a great result, I would recommend that you see a Facial Plastic Surgeon, Occuloplastic Surgeon, or Plastic Surgeon. I have seen great work from Surgeons representing each of the specialties, and some terrible work

There are many factors that go into how much one pays for a blepharoplasty and many of the answers have already gone into them, so I won’t repeat them. One final piece of the pie is overhead. That is, rent in some parts of the country may be twice what they are elsewhere. I know that nurses in California make twice as much as they do in some other states, so your surgeon is paying rent, salaries, malpractice insurance, etc

All these vary widely state by state. Now, if there is a huge variability within one city, I would always worry about the person doing it on the cheap. Do they have the same safety standards in their operating room

Is the operating room certified, the anesthesiologis certified, and the physician certified in plastic surgery?

“Skin Pinch” Blepharoplasty

The so-called “Skin-Pinch” Blepharoplasty involves removal of a small amount of skin from just under the lash margin. This procedure is designed to remove excess lower eyelid skin and decrease the complications of a traditional lower blepharoplasty

Doctor Cory Torgerson Blepharoplasty Before And After Young Patient

Many times this procedure is combined with a sub-conjunctival removal of fat to address the lower eyelid “bags”.

What to do after an orbital fracture

If you had a trauma that caused a fracture of the bones around the eye, it is possible that the eye is sinking backward. In most cases this can be improved with surgery. The first step is to see a surgeon who has experience with orbital trauma such as an oculoplastic surgeon

Your doctor will want to get a CT to see what is happening with the bones around the eye. Surgery can be more difficult when the problem has been there for a while but it is still possible most of the time

Lower blepharoplasty

You will benefit from a lower blepharoplasty. In your consultations you may hear different techniques recommended and that leads to confusion. However, there are different ways to solve the problem and you need to get comfortable with working with a surgeon and have trust in them to solve the problem

SMOKING AND COSMETIC Face and eyelid surgery.

You can have surgery and will likely do just as well as anyone who never smoked. You present general health is the issue. If you are now healthy surgery will go fine. You past smoking should be an almost non issue

Most optimal treatment for droopy, sagging eye lids?

While there are several options for eye rejuvenation, it is hard to say exactly what you would need. It could be removing the upper eye skin; it could be a browlift. The best suggestion for you is to see a facial plastic surgeon and be evaluated in person.

Sunken lower lids, best treatment?

Your best option would to use a hyaluronic acid filler like Restylane. Ther’s no down time with this injection and can last up to 9 months

The key is to find the surgeon or dermatologist who has a vast experience injecting around the eyes. A more permanent option would be autologous fat injections to blend the lid-cheek junction.

Lower lid Blepharoplasty

it appears that you are a good candidate for a lower lid blepharoplasty to remove the excess fat.

It may be the respective opinions of the surgeons you went to – a preference in doing lower lid blepharoplasty with how they trained or the results they tend to get doing it one way or the other

I would suggest going to the surgeon who you feel the most comfortable with. Transconjunctival blepharoplasty is considered by most (oculoplastics surgeons at least) as being the more evolved or modern way of removing fat from the lower eyelids
