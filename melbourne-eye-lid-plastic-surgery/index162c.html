Melbourne Eye Lid Plastic Surgery

Eyelid surgery (blepharoplasty) in Melbourne is a tightening of the skin around the eyes to get rid of sagging skin of the upper eyelid and lower eyelid.

This procedure eliminates wrinkles around the eyes. Local or general anesthesia is used during surgery, depending on the complexity of plastic surgery.

Eyelid surgery is performed by laser or traditional manner by a small incision and lifting. Laser blepharoplasty has more advantages. The seam is so thin that even the scar becomes invisible after a period of recovery. Eyelid surgery is the most in demand, which carries long-lasting results.

Indications. Eyelid surgery (Blepharoplasty).

– weary eyes (“heavy” eyelids);
– “Bags” under the eyes;
– excess skin and fatty tissue around the eyelids;
– overhang skin of the upper eyelid ;
– signs of age-related changes around the eyelids;
– the desire to change the shape of the eye;
– eliminate congenital and acquired defects of eyelids.

Blepharoplasty significantly improves the appearance of the face, slows down signs of aging in the eye area, giving a significant rejuvenating effect for a long time.

Eyelid Surgery involves the removal of excess skin and fatty tissue in the area of the upper and lower eyelids.

Therefore, blepharoplasty are divided into types: upper, lower and circular blepharoplasty.

Techniques of blepharoplasty:

  1. Transconjunctival eyelid surgery.
  2. Transcutaneous eyelid surgery.
  3. Upper eyelids surgery.

Upper Blepharoplasty is aimed at remove excess skin and fat through an incision in the natural crease of the eyelid. The incision is closed with a thin seam which then is invisible.

Upper eyelid surgery takes about 1 hour in outpatient basis. Cosmetic sutures are removed after 7 days. This plastic surgery can be performed in combination with the brows lift to reduce skin excision on the eyelids.

Lower eyelids surgery

Lower blepharoplasty in Melbourne is aimed at the removal of fat in the lower eyelids. They can be removed or moved through an incision on the inner surface of the eyelid.

Simultaneously are performed laser dermabrasion, contour plastic, chemical peels or the use of Botox injections to smooth and tighten the lower eyelid skin.

An incision is made just below the lashes if there is excess skin in the lower eyelids.

Circular blepharoplasty

The upper and lower blepharoplasty are performed together and can be combined with other procedures, such as eyebrow surgery or forehead lift, midface lifting or laser skin resurfacing.

Transconjunctival eyelid surgery.

Transconjunctival blepharoplasty in Melbourne is one of the approaches to surgery of the lower eyelid. This method is used for more than 90% cases. This method allows to provide easy and safe access to the fat, which can be removed or moved over the cheekbones.
