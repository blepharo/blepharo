Colorado Springs, CO Surgery For Sagging Eyelids

Blepharoplasty, or eyelid surgery in Colorado Springs is a surgical procedure that involves removing excess skin of the lower and upper eyelids, aimed at correcting the eyelids, with the aim of improving.

This concept includes the correction of the upper and lower eyelids, the circular blepharoplasty, transconjunctival blepharoplasty.

Types of blepharoplasty.

Modern blepharoplasty has several popular destinations.

Upper Blepharoplasty is performed on the upper eyelid.

Lower blepharoplasty (classic or traditional) is performed on the lower eyelid, incisions are made directly under the lash line.

Transconjunctival blepharoplasty in Colorado Springs is an effective method for removal of hernias of the lower eyelid.

Circular blepharoplasty performed on both eyes. Laser blepharoplasty is a modern, non-traumatic procedure in which a thin incisions are performed by a medical laser.

Laser blepharoplasty is performed under local anesthesia.

It is suitable for patients with satisfactory condition of the skin eyelids.

Indications for blepharoplasty

Indications for surgical blepharoplasty are age-related changes, as well as the disadvantages, which are due to the anatomical structure of the eyelids.

The main reasons for recourse to the surgeon the following:

  1. the formation of excess eyelid skin (the overhang effect);
  2. herniation of the upper and especially the lower eyelid;
  3. wrinkles around the eyes and eyelids;
  4. the lower corners of the eyes are omitted.

There is no age limit for blepharoplasty in Colorado Springs. Experts do not advise to do blepharoplasty to minor patients, because at this age comes to an end the process of forming the shape and magnitude of the eyes.

In exceptional cases, transconjunctival blepharoplasty (seamless) can be performed recommended by the physician.

Contraindications to blepharoplasty.

This surgical procedure has a number of contraindications, we list the main of them are:

  1. Increased intraocular pressure.
  2. Blood sugar level is above normal.
  3. Violation of the thyroid gland.
  4. Hypertension.
  5. Diseases of the heart.
  6. Bronchial asthma.
  7. Violation of the integrity of the skin around the eyes.
  8. Oncology.
  9. Exacerbation of infectious diseases.
  10. Diseases of the eyelids and the presence of conjunctivitis in the chronic form, are also considered as contraindications to blepharoplasty.

Preparation for the procedure.

Preparation for the procedure starts with the first consultation of the surgeon. Patient may to tell about it wishes, and plastic surgeons will inform him about the technique and the possible outcome of the forthcoming eye lid operation.

Approximately two weeks prior to plastic surgery you need to stop taking medicines that increase bleeding. Is required to undergo a thorough medical examination to detect contraindications for corrective eyelid surgery.

The surgeon selects the technique of blepharoplasty, after receiving preliminary results of a patient survey. The surgery to lift eyelids takes 1-2.5 hours, depending on its complexity.

The procedure is divided into several stages:

Anesthesia. The choice of anesthesia, used in this procedure, depending on its complexity, so to the patient is injected a local, general anesthesia or is used intravenous sedation.

Operating incisions. After the start of the anesthesia, the plastic surgeon makes the desired incisions, which are in the natural folds and helps hide the lines thereafter.

Closing of the incisions. After conducting all necessary surgical manipulations, the surgeon that performs blepharoplasty, closes the incisions in the eyelids. For this purpose used:

  1. bioresorbable or removable surgical materials (thread);
  2. glue for the skin;
  3. special surgical tape.

A few days after the cosmetic surgery eyes, the eyes should be closed to preserve the integrity of the seams.

Sensations during and after eyelid surgery.

Typically, blepharoplasty is performed under local anesthesia, using modern powerful anesthetic that will create a good analgesic effect. In some cases, general anesthesia may be combined with a local anesthesia.
