Cosmetic Blepharoplasty In Virginia Beach, VA

Eyelid surgery in Virginia Beach is one of the key operations, allowing to achieve a significant rejuvenating effect, as perception of the person largely determines his eyes.

Age-related decline in tissue elasticity and saturation with water are rather quickly reflected on the eyelids, the skin of which is very thin. The so-called fat hernias arise due to loss of elasticity of eyelid skin, representing of the bulging fat. These hernias with flabby and saggy skin can add to the face ten years.

On this basis, the essence of blepharoplasty is a surgical removal of excess skin and hernias of the upper and lower eyelids. Do not confuse the true tissue changes that require intervention with a temporary phenomenon such as “tired eyes” or “bags under the eyes” as a result of fatigue, lack of sleep, etc.

Often, kidney disease and cardiovascular system lead to such changes. All this is requires different treatments. Typically, blepharoplasty is done to people (mostly to women) aged 35 years and older, but the eye tightening surgery can be carried out also at a younger age in the presence of problems.

Blepharoplasty in Virginia Beach is not recommended for people with high blood pressure, or dry eye, as well as thyroid disease, cardiovascular system and diabetes.

During the planning of the eye surgery plastic, you need to understand a few key points. In patients 30-40 years old, without significant changes in related areas, the result of blepharoplasty is generally well forecast. At the same time, at an older age with concomitant ptosis of upper or midface, an isolated intervention only on the eyelids can not solve fully all the problems, especially when a significant drooping eyebrows.

Also, if blepharoplasty will performed too cardinally, it may cause a certain disharmony – a youthful eyes at the background of the overhanging brow and cheeks.

The upper, or (and) lower blepharoplasty is performed, depending on the age-related changes, which can be combined with a forehead lift and / or eyebrows, and complemented by non-surgical methods of correction. All issues about blepharoplasty are usually discussed at the consultation.

These operations assumes quite a serious intervention in the area of orbital fat. Therefore, before eyelid surgery in Virginia Beach the patient usually have a full medical examination and receives the necessary consultation of experts, in particular an ophthalmologist.

You will receive the necessary recommendations to the preoperative period in preparation for the eyelid cosmetic surgery. You need to plan your work schedule in order to have free time 7-14 days on primary recovery.

Dr Kevin Bounds Blepharoplasty Lower Recovery Candidate Images Result

Blepharoplasty is performed in stationary conditions. Anaesthesia is almost always general, complemented with local infiltration anesthesia.

On the upper eyelid, the incisions are in such a way that when the eyes open, postoperative scars are invisible.

On the lower eyelid, an incision is made just below the eyelash line. Through these incisions, the skin is separated from the underlying tissue, excess fat, muscle and skin are removed.

After that the incisions are sutured with a cosmetic sutures. Transconjunctival blepharoplasty can be performed at a young age, if there is no a significant excess skin of the lower eyelids.

In this method, an incision is made inside the lower eyelid and does not leave any traces on the outside. The surgeon apply a bandage after the eyelid droop surgery, which in most cases is removed the next day.

Dr Richard Rosenblum Upper Blepharoplasty Recovery

The duration of hospital stay is usually no more than 1-2 days. During the first days after the eyelid surgery in Virginia Beach is recommended to do cold compresses to reduce swelling and bruising.

Postoperative pain is mild and does not require a special anesthetizing. Eye drops may be assigned for the first few days. Sutures are removed at 5-7 days.

Not recommended a heavy loads that can cause additional bleeding due to increased blood pressure (including – hot showers, trunk bending, exercise, etc.) during the first 4 weeks.

The tissue swelling and bruising are gradually go down for 2-3 weeks. The final results are estimated in 1,5 months. Keep in mind that healing is a gradual process, and scars may remain slightly pinkish for 6 months, but eventually they will be almost invisible. Insolation is not recommended for 3-6 months in order to avoid darkening of scars.
