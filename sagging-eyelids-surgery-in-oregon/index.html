Your photo shows fullness of your lower lids, which is hereditary by your history. Your question is a really interesting one because you are rightly concerned about the overly respected look your aunt developed as she aged after her lower lids were corrected surgically

Things in plastic surgery have changed over time. We have come to understand that the aging of the face involves not just loosening and wrinkling of the skin and pouching out of fat in places where we don’t want it, like your lower lids

Aging also involves shifting of facial fat from high in the cheeks to low in the jowls for instance. Traditionally, we saw fat like your lower lids and wanted to take it out. At the time that your aunt had her surgery, lower lid procedures were probably done through an incision on the outside of the lid, just under the eyelashes

This approach also caused scarring that frequently distorted the lower lid as it healed and as it continued to age. Now, you would be treated with an incision from inside the lid, directly removing fat without distorting the the rest of the lid and without leaving a visible scar

This would definitely improve your tear trough visibility. It is also the case that over time, plastic surgeons have recognized the importance of fat, and we take out less than we used to so patients are less likely to look hollowed out than they used to

That having been said, as you age you are likely to volume in your cheeks below your tear troughs, whether through gravity or through the natural loss of fat that occurs with aging and so your tear troughs may become a problem again

I actually recommend fresh pineapple, as it contains a good amount of Bromelain. Try to eat as much pineapple (not canned) as possible leading up to the procedure. If you have already purchased Arnica, this can be beneficial as well

There are various brands of Arnica, so I recommend following the manufacturers instructions. Typically the tablets would be taken 3 times a day until complete and some suggest beginning three days prior.

How to reduce bruising after a blepharoplasty

The best advice that I can give you is to avoid any multivitamins, aspirin, aspirin containing products, and/or blood thinners for two weeks prior to your blepharoplasty procedure in Oregon. For patients who are currently on aspirin or blood thinning regimens as per their medical doctor, I always advise that they defer to their medical doctor regarding the avoidance of these medications before doing so

In my practice, I do recommend that patients begin taking oral arnica and bromelain a few days prior to and following their surgical procedure in an effort to prevent and shorten the duration of any bruising that a patient may experience, and remember that head elevation and cool compresses for the first 24-48 hours should also help

Unfortunately, bruising is highly variable and unpredictable.
