Eyelid surgery complications

Although generally safe, eyelid surgery should not be taken lightly. There are potential complications that should be considered before undertaking eyelid surgery. The most common complication is asymmetry of appearance, especially in the upper eyelids

One cannot expect one side to look exactly like the mirror image of the other side. Even in the unoperated situation, many people’s eyelids do not look exactly like the mirror image of the other side

However the asymmetry can sometimes be accentuated after surgery. Usually this condition can be corrected with revisional surgery. If too much skin is removed from the eyelids, ectopion, or outward pulling of the eyelid can result

Excess tearing can result after eyelid surgery. Redness in the conjunctiva of the eye or small water blisters can occur. Swelling and bruising are common immediately after eyelid surgery. Blindness is a very unusual complication
