Blepharoplasty in Sydney is a surgical procedure to correct the appearance of the upper and lower eyelids. It allows you to get rid of wrinkles and overhanging folds, so that the face looks younger to several years.

Blepharoplasty is often performed at 35-40 years after of age deformation. During this period, the skin and the muscle fibers are losing their elasticity and flexibility that reduces the overall tone.

Indications for the Eyelid Surgery

1. Correction of incision or the shape of the eye to improve appearance;
2. The overhanging skin fold located on the upper eyelid;
3. Excessive amounts of the skin in the form of “bags” under the eyes;
4. Dropping eye corners that change facial expression;
5. Congenital and acquired defects of the upper or lower eyelids.

Contraindications to the Eyelid Surgery

  • Acute and chronic infectious diseases, including eye (blepharitis, conjunctivitis, etc…);
  • Diabetes and diseases of the blood coagulation system;
  • Pregnancy and breast-feeding;
  • It is recent damage to the skin of the upper and lower eyelids in the form of abrasions, scratches or bruises.

The process of blepharoplasty

Blepharoplasty can be performed either under general anesthesia or under local anesthesia, it is discussed at the consultation. Overhanging and the loose skin on the upper eyelids are excised.

A sterile bandage is imposed after suturing of wound. Blepharoplasty is performed so that incisions are in the natural folds of the upper eyelids, and they are invisible after healing.

This operation is called transconjunctival blepharoplasty. Due to the fact that there is no incision under the lash line (as in the classic blepharoplasty), scars will not remain.
