What is a blepharoplasty?

This type of cosmetic surgery is the excision of excess skin and fatty hernias, which are formed on the upper and lower eyelids. The defect is not only visual. Bags under the eyes reduce peripheral vision, which can complicate life for patients driving a car.

Surgical intervention is performed both on four eyelids simultaneously, and on the two upper or lower eyelids separately. Modern plastic surgeons successfully combine blepharoplasty with cervico-facial and frontal lifting, as well as with peelings and dermabrasion.

Most often, cosmetic surgery eye lift in Boise is performed in the following cases:

– the upper eyelid hangs and turns into a heavy ugly crease;
– lower eyelid aging and turns into a bag under the eye;
– stretching the skin around the eyes leads to the appearance of deep wrinkles;
– eyelids with fatty hernias, constantly swollen and reddened;
– excess skin of the upper eyelid interferes with vision and application of cosmetics.

In the process of surgical eye lift, the surgeon removes excess skin, fat and muscle tissue, without disturbing the function of the eyelids. Quite often, blepharoplasty is performed in conjunction with an endoscopic forehead lift.

Also, non-surgical blepharoplasty is gaining popularity, being performed according to innovative methods. The non-surgical method of eyelid surgery is sparing and gives results similar to surgical interventions.

Preparation for blepharoplasty in Boise

First of all, the surgeon finds out from the patient whether previous operations have been performed on the eyelids, whether she has a predisposition to diabetes and hypertension, whether she adheres to an active and healthy lifestyle.
It is also important to inform the doctor about the presence of hypothyroidism, vision problems, use of antidepressants and hypnotics, glaucoma and increased intraocular pressure, because in these cases blepharoplasty is contraindicated.

At the next stage of preparation, the patient undergoes a clinical examination, which includes determining the exact amount of excess skin, the presence of superficial or deep wrinkles, the direction of the outer third of the eyelids, condition of its fibroelastic tissue, and degree of risk ptosis the lower eyelid.

Before and after surgery, the patient should take decongestants for a week and completely exclude the use of drugs containing aspirin.

Blepharoplasty is performed under local anesthesia (injection into the eyelid region), deep local (intravenously with a tranquilizer) and general anesthesia (the patient is asleep).

For older patients, a laser method is prescribed, performed by a special device that allows the operation to be bloodless and to shorten the rehabilitation period to a minimum.
