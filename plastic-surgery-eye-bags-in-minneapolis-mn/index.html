Plastic Surgery Eye Bags In Minneapolis, MN

Aging of the face is a natural process that proceeds gradually. The skin and deeper tissues sag with the years, wrinkles and wrinkles appear on the face.

This phenomenon is especially noticeable in the eye area, where wrinkles and folds of the skin on the upper and lower eyelids are formed.

Excess adipose tissue protruding outwards, heavier eyelids, and promote the formation of “bags” under the eyes. One way to make the face younger is a blepharoplasty (eyelid correction) in Minneapolis.

Its task is to remove excess skin and subcutaneous fat from the eyelids and, in essence, tighten the skin around the eyes.

This operation does not eliminate wrinkles in the corners of the eyes and does not raise the hanging eyebrows.

For these purposes, the skin of the forehead is tightened. During the initial consultation the doctor will explain to you how the operation will be performed and what are its possible complications.

Then the doctor will take a picture of your face in different projections. There is no age limit for blepharoplasty, but it should be remembered that with age, the elasticity of the skin decreases and, accordingly, the effectiveness of the procedure decreases.

Preparation for blepharoplasty in Minneapolis

Before the plastic surgery for eyes, you will need to take blood tests (total and coagulation analysis). If necessary, you will need to undergo additional testing (ECG, chest X-ray, vision, lacrimal fluid analysis, etc.).

You need to take care of the presence of painkillers at home: optalgin in tablets or drops (if you do not have allergies to this drug). 6 hours before surgery, you should not eat or drink.

You can use chewing gum. On the day of surgery, you should wear comfortable clothes and shoes (preferably a blouse or shirt with a front clasp).

Minneapolis blepharoplasty is performed under local anesthesia with strong sedatives, so you will not experience pain. Perhaps a feeling of tension and pressure in the eyeballs, when the surgeon will remove the subcutaneous adipose tissue.

An anesthesiologist will install an intravenous catheter through which drugs for anesthesia will be injected. During plastic surgery for eyes, the surgeon will perform an incision along the crease of the upper eyelid and on the lower eyelid, along the line of the eyelashes.

Excess skin of the upper eyelid is removed in accordance with the scheme prepared before under eye lift. Excess fatty tissue is also removed. The incision in the lower eyelid is made under the eyelashes.

After eyelid surgery under eyes, the skin incision is closed and taped over with a thin plaster. Suture removal is carried out in 3-4 days.

Postoperative period

At the end of the eyelid surgery in Minneapolis, you will be transferred to a postoperative ward to awaken from anesthesia. You will be in the ward for a few hours. Medical personnel will constantly monitor the state of your consciousness, breathing, blood pressure and oxygen level, pulse rate and other indicators.
After awakening, you will feel discomfort on the operated area.

Recovery period

3-4 days after discharge from the clinic, you will need to come to the first examination to the doctor to remove the stitches. After the blepharoplasty, it is recommended to wash your hair every day.

The next day after the baggy eye surgery, swelling and bruising appear. These symptoms reach their peak by the second day. Subsequently, swelling is reduced, bruising moves to the cheek area and gradually change color, and then disappear. This process can take up to two weeks.

In the early days it is recommended to sleep and lie with a raised head. In a few days you can leave the house, already with make-up and necessarily wearing sunglasses. You should avoid physical strain and sharp head tilt down.

In the first days it is recommended to take painkillers before the pain becomes too strong. Every six hours you can take optalgin (in tablets or in drops). If before the end of six hours you feel severe pain, take an additional pill of acamole. Do not take aspirin or non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAID). These medications can cause bleeding.
