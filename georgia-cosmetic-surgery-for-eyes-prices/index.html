Georgia Cosmetic Surgery For Eyes Prices

Georgia blepharoplasty involves removal of the eyelid skin, removal or tightening of the underlying orbicularis muscle, and removal or shaping of some orbital fat.

Key to good surgery is preserving the natural looking appearance of the eyelids

This requires varying degree of surgery and shaping of the upper eyelid, including each of these anatomical layers depending on a person’s individual face, ethnicity and gender. Removal of excessive skin and soft tissues can result in an unnatural appearance and troublesome dry eyes

In addition removal of excessive orbital fat can change a person’s appearance by hollowing out the upper eyelid rather than rejuvenating one’s appearance the upper eyelid is more aged, this is sometimes termed skeletonized appearance upper eyelid

Often to get the best results, removal of soft tissues is not sufficient, additional procedures such as skin resurfacing, the use of botulinum toxin, correction of droopy upper eyelid (ptosis), fat transfer amongst others will all have a role in natural rejuvenation

Anesthesia for Blepharoplasty

Although lower eyelid blepharoplasty is possible under local anesthesia alone, I would not recommend it. Upper eyelid procedures are fine to do under local. It is more of a surface procedure and patient comfort is OK

Even a skin pinch procedure is difficult for patients under local anesthesia. If fat removal is also needed, then sedation is clearly the way to go. The lower eyelids and eyelid fat are just too sensitive to do under local anesthesia alone

In my experience, procedures are less effective in uncomfortable patients. I would want you to have the best outcome and would strongly recommend a sedation or twilight procedure for any lower eyelid procedure other than skin pinch alone
