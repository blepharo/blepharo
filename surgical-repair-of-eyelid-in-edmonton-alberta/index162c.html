Complications of blepharoplasty

After blepharoplasty surgery in Edmonton, there are occasionally minor setbacks that temporarily affect the patient during the first week or two of the healing process.

These issues can include pain, chemosis or irritation of the eyes, subconjunctival hemorrhage, bruising around the eyes, excessive tearing, swelling of the lids, and initial appearance of asymmetry of the eyelids

Fortunately major complications are much less common but can also occur. They can include immediate post-surgical issues such as hematoma formation, infection of the surrounding skin or the eye itself, double vision, lacrimal duct injury, corneal abrasion, or breakdown of the incision

Certain cosmetic complications may also become apparent during the healing process. These may include residual excessive eyelid skin, eyelid ptosis (drooping), dry eyes, eyelid crease asymmetry, eyelid retraction/ectropion, lagopthalmos (incomplete closure of eye), and a superior sulcus deformity (depression over the eye)
