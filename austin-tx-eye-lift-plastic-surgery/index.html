Rejuvenation of the upper and lower eyelids.

The upper eyelids

Before the blepharoplasty, the surgeon makes a marking on the eyelids. The upper eyelids are marked in lying position with open eyes. Marking is done at the lower edge of the overhanging folds located at 9 -11 mm higher than the lash line.

During the eyelid surgery, the surgeon removes a strip of skin marked. If necessary, strip the circular muscles of the eye may be removed. The surgeon performs the control of bleeding and sutured the wound.

The lower eyelids

An incision is made under the eyelashes at a distance of 2-3 mm from the edge and continues 5-9 mm beyond the outer corner of the eye at medium “crow’s feet”.

Excess adipose tissue is removed or moved to the lacrimal groove. An important stage of the surgical eye lift is to determine the borders of the excision of excess skin of the lower eyelid. The skin flap is pulled when the patient’s eyes open, and he looks upward.

Excess flap are excised with muscle or without. When closing a wound on the muscle is sometimes applied resorbable suture, and on the skin applied intradermal suture.

In some cases, when there is the weakness of the outer edge of the lower eyelid when omitted corners of the eyes, perform the lifting the outer edge of the eyelid and fixing its to the periosteum.

This manipulation prevents postoperative complication such as an eversion of the lower eyelid (contraction).
