What is a blepharoplasty?

Blepharoplasty in Ohio is plastic surgery to remove excess skin and fat from the upper or lower eyelid, or a change in eye shape.

With the help of blepharoplasty is possible to get rid of fatty hernia (bags), to lift the corners of the eyes, to make the eyes a little more, or to change their shape.

In Europe, women aged 45-50 years, more often resorted to blepharoplasty in Ohio, who want to restore youth and slightly tighten the eyelid skin.

In Asia, this operation is often carried out in the very young age of 18-20 years. The main goal of asian blepharoplasty to change the shape of the eyes and make them visually larger and more rounded.

Indications for eyelid surgery.

Indications for blepharoplasty in Ohio are:

  1. ptosis (drooping) of the upper eyelid;
  2. excess skin of the upper or lower eyelids;
  3. excess fat under the eyes, the fat herniation;
  4. the eyes shape, which interferes with normal vision (however, if the patients simply are not satisfied with the natural shape of the eye, it is also an occasion to address to the surgeon);
  5. bags under the eyes;
  6. laxity of the muscles around the eyes;
  7. deep wrinkles around the eyes;
  8. exophthalmos – protrusion of the eyeball;
  9. the asymmetry of the eyes;
  10. entropion eyelids;
  11. damage to the eyelid skin due to injury and birth defects.

How is a surgical blepharoplasty performed?

Blepharoplasty of upper eyelids.

This is not a particularly difficult operation for an experienced surgeon. However, blepharoplasty is always a jewelry work. The physician makes an incision along the line the natural skin fold and removes excess skin and fat tissue and forms the upper lid, if necessary.

The whole operation takes 1.5 to 3 hours and is generally carried out under general anesthesia, although in some cases is acceptable the local anesthesia.

Blepharoplasty of lower eyelids.

Blepharoplasty of lower eyelids is more complicated and requires special skill of the surgeon. The incision in this case is made directly under the lash line, in the but otherwise process is similar to the upper blepharoplasty.

Transconjunctival blepharoplasty.

Transconjunctival blepharoplasty is performed only for the removal of fat hernia under the eyes and only for young patients whose skin has retained its elasticity. The surgeon makes an incision on the inner surface of the lower eyelid, removes fat and sutured, seam will be completely invisible after healing.

Recovery and potential complications.

You should not expect a a striking effect immediately after eyelid surgery in Ohio. In the first few days of the seams are red and inflamed, and around the eyes is strong enough swelling.

Eyelid Surgery Healing Photos With Dr Jean Loftus

However, do not be afraid. The rehabilitation period takes about 10-14 days and after this time it will be possible to evaluate the effect. Unfortunately, the pain can not be avoided, but it is possible to stop with analgesics.

You will have to comply with doctor’s advice until healing wounds. It is forbidden to rub or scratch your eyes, it is better not to touch them at all. Do not wear contact lenses and to strain your eyes – will have to do without TV, computer, and even books. You will also need abandon the smoking and alcohol, do not apply make-up and to clean the eyes very carefully.

You can not go out without a high-quality sunglasses for a month after the surgery.

If you comply with these rules, and the cosmetic under eye surgery was successful, the effect will delight you 7-15 years.

However, you should know about possible complications.

Edema is not considered a complication, but if it persists for a long time is a is cause for concern. Other complications of blepharoplasty include subcutaneous hematoma, and located behind the eyeball.

Infections and divergence of seams are quite common complications, which in most cases are not caused by the negligence of a doctor, but the patient’s carelessness, which does not comply with the rules of post-operative care.

A situation when after the eye lid lift surgery the patient can not completely close the eyes, as a result they dry up and become inflamed. This is not a complication, and medical error. Alas, this happens quite often, if people are wanting to save money, turn to dubious “experts.”
