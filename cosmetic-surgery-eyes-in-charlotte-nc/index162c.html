Cosmetic Surgery Eyes In Charlotte, NC

Blepharoplasty in Charlotte, NC is performed to eliminate the shape correction of eyelids and removal of excess skin and fat of the upper and lower eyelids.

Blepharoplasty can be performed on the upper and the lower eyelids, as well as on both eyelids simultaneously.

Eyelid surgery is often performed in combination with other procedures, in particular, face lift, as well as various forms of face contour correction, laser resurfacing of the skin and peeling.

One of the most common types of blepharoplasty is lifting of the lower eyelid when it hanging in form bag. This procedure is called canthoplasty.

It is used to correct blepharoptosis (ptosis of the eyelids).

Among other things, blepharoplasty performed in the case of common age-related skin changes in the face, when the eyelids are similar to bags under the eyes.

Today there are several types of blepharoplasty in a plastic surgeon’s arsenal, such as laser blepharoplasty, Asian blepharoplasty, transconjunctival blepharoplasty, fat redistribution eyelids.

Indications for blepharoplasty.

Blepharoplasty in Charlotte, NC is often carried out at the first signs of aging, when the skin loses its tone and its overhanging occurs in the forehead area, nasolabial folds, eyelids, eyebrows and neck.

Blepharoplasty, especially in combination with other procedures, allows to rejuvenate the face and remove from the face of fatigue.

Among the most frequent indications for blepharoplasty are such defects in shape eyelids, like plump, sacciform upper eyelids with excess skin in the form of creases, drooping of the lower eyelid in the form of a bag as a result of old age, swollen “fat” folds in the upper and lower eyelid, and and fat herniation, which is reason for the formation of bags under the eyes, wrinkles of the eyelids due to loss of skin tone, eye fatigue associated with drooping upper lids which interfere with normal vision.

Preparing for blepharoplasty

Before performing eye lid operation and choosing of a particular method of blepharoplasty, the doctor carries examination of the patient. First of all, the doctor ask of the patient about past illnesses, especially diseases of the eye, as well as surgeries, including plastic surgeries on the face.

Then are carried out blood and urine tests, both general and biochemical as well as tests for blood clotting and blood test for sugar, ECG to evaluate the heart function.

Should also to inform the doctor about taking any medications, and if you have allergies on medications and especially to local anesthetics.

The doctor also assesses the nature of the visual acuity of the patient, whether he uses glasses or contact lenses, and suffers from dry eye syndrome, and using artificial tear.

Furthermore, if there are concomitant diseases such as glaucoma, the consultation of ophthalmologist is required. Next, the surgeon assesses the shape deformation of eyelids, to choose the appropriate surgical technique.

The surgeon determines the amount of excess skin on the eyelids, the direction of the outer third of the eyelid and the extent of the possible ptosis of the eyelid. The doctor also assesses the state of the depth of wrinkles, the tone of the eyelids, as well as the tension of the lower eyelid forward.

In this case the lid is pulled to 1 cm and returns to its original position. Next, the surgeon slightly pressing on the eye estimates the amount of excess fat tissue. Next, he determines the function of the muscles that raise the upper eyelid and the presence of senile ptosis eyelid (senile ptosis).

How is blepharoplasty performed?

Charlotte, NC blepharoplasty lasts on average from 1 to 3 hours, or even longer, depending on the interference volume (one or all four of the eyelid), as well as combination with other procedures.

The incision at the upper blepharoplasty is usually done in the area of the upper eyelid crease to hide the postoperative scar. Conversely, when the incision at the lower blepharoplasty is made almost on the edge of the eyelid, just below the lashes, so as to hide the scar.

However, more often during lower lid blepharoplasty, the incision is carried out not on the outside of the eyelid, and on the inside. It is transconjunctival incision, ie, through the conjunctiva.

It allows to remove excess fatty tissue of the lower eyelid. The surface of the eye is protected by a plastic lid in the shape of a contact lens during eye cosmetic surgery.

The surgeon removes the excess fatty tissue primarily and corrects the underlying muscles of the eyelids. Further is carried excision of excess skin that usually hangs. Next, the surgeon perform “resurfacing” the remainder of the eyelid skin and the incision is sutured.

Usually, before making the incisions, the doctor makes the lines of markup, on which the incisions will be carried out. The choice the lines of markup depends, of course, from the choice of a particular method of blepharoplasty by a plastic surgeon.

The choice of anesthesia for blepharoplasty depends on many factors – the general condition of the patient, his preferences and the lack of contraindications.

The postoperative period after blepharoplasty in Charlotte, NC

After cosmetic surgery on eyes, the patient will feeling pain, unpleasant sensations in the form of heavy eyelids, and some eye irritation. Usually the first time, a sterile dressing is applied on the eyelids, which changes periodically.

Swelling and bruising can occur after blepharoplasty. Immediately after surgical eye lift, a cold compress, in the form of ice packs is superimposed on the eyelids to reduce postoperative edema.

In general, the recovery period after blepharoplasty is up to 2 weeks. Also, the doctor will advise you to avoid wearing contact lenses during the early postoperative period.

Makeup can be applied also in about two weeks. Most often thin absorbable sutures are used for blepharoplasty. They need not be removed. Postoperative scars after blepharoplasty in Charlotte, NC are in places of natural folds of the eyelids, so that they almost barely discernible.

Bruising around the eyes are absorbed in a week or two. In addition, the next day after the eye surgery should be carried out special exercises for the eyes. In addition, it is strongly recommended to limit TV viewing and the use of alcohol and smoking during recovery period, as it is may aggravate course of the process of wound healing.
