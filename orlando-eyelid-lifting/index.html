Blepharoplasty In Orlando, FL is an operation aimed at removing excess skin and fatty tissue in the area of the upper and lower eyelids.

This operation allows to eliminate bags under the eyes, folds and wrinkles around the eyes, but does not remove the small wrinkles in infraorbital area, wrinkles in the outer corners of the eyes (“crow’s feet”), and on the forehead.

Eyelid surgery is used for improving the appearance of the whole, and to eliminate the signs of aging in this area. Blepharoplasty can be often combined with the circular facial plastic surgery, laser resurfacing and other treatments.

Blepharoplasty in Orlando is usually performed in a day hospital or hospitalization for one day. There are a number of aesthetic blepharoplasty techniques.

Methods of operations may differ greatly in different situations, depending on the prevailing conditions, the severity of the signs of aging, excess skin and fatty tissue.

Classical techniques of blepharoplasty involve incisions upper and lower eyelids, which are located along the natural folds and removal of excess skin and fatty tissue protrusion.

Scars remaining after blepharoplasty are in the natural folds and almost invisible.

The seam of the upper eyelid is located in the so-called palpebral crease and with eyes open is not visible, suture of the lower eyelid also masked under the natural anatomical formations of the area. After some time after the operation the scars fade and almost disappear.
