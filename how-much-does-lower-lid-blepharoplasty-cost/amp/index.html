Finding A Doctor To Perform Lower Blepharoplasty Surgery

There are some questions to consider when consulting an eyelid expert about lower lid surgery and cost.

You should ask if the surgeon is approach the eye bags or the eyelid skin on the lower eyelid. You should also ask if they will addressing the laxity of the lower lid and the heaviness of the midface/cheek. Finally you should ask if they are skilled in transpositioning of the eye fat bags towards the tear trough to help fill eye hollows or if they excise the fat.

Once all of these questions are answered, pricing will make more sense. You should find an eyelid expert who focuses on this area through years of training and a focus around the eyes. Pricing is regional but ask detailed questions to know what you are paying for. (Sean Paul, MD, Austin Oculoplastic Surgeon)

Lower Lid Surgery Can Drastically Minimize The Appearance Of Baggy And Puffy Lower Eyelids

Asking how much a blepharoplasty costs is like asking how much a car costs: a 1992 Mazda Miata was sexy at the time but is worth nothing now, it’s styling is obsolete and technology has improved leaps and bounds. 

The same is true for blepharoplasty surgery, technique and aesthetics have improved dramatically, for the better, when compared to 20 and even 10 years ago. Old school blepharoplasty technique involved external, skin and muscle incisions with excess removal of fat and had poor long term results and adverse effects: hollowness and disruption of normal eyelid position from scarring. Newer technique have no external incisions and perform all work through the inside of the eye, with fat repositioning instead of removal, which improves the cosmetic outcomes and nearly eliminates the eyelid position issues. 

This technique is called transconjunctival blepharoplasty, and most well-trained, modern surgeons prefer this technique. One area that you can save some cost is on the anesthesia. Most well-trained, modern era surgeons should be more than comfortable performing this under local anesthesia only. I perform almost all of these procedures this way with pure comfort on the patients end, and this increases first and foremost the safety of the the procedure, the recovery ease, time spent at clinic, and of course cost.
