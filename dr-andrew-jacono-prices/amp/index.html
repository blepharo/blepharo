How to treat under eye bags

There are many approaches to treating lower eyelid bags. In young patients like yourself this problem can often be treated entirely via volume enhancement of the cheek/lower eyelid (using fat grafting or facial filler such as restylane for example)

Depending on how prominent the lower eyelid fat pads are I may recommend concurrent conservative removal of some of the fat via a transconjuctival approach, but this is less common. I find that if the cheek volume loss isn’t addressed the result will be more of a hollowed out look, which isn’t as natural appearing

Transcutaneous lower blepharoplasty has risks

Transcutaneous has inherent risks of causing lower eyelid retraction, rounding of the eyes, etc.

You can still address the skin using skin pinch technique in addition to transconjunctival technique to address the bags. See an oculoplastic surgeon.

Revision Blepharoplasty with Remaining Fat

It is in fact possible to conservatively remove a small amount of remaining fat, without needing to do a lot of surgery.

Ex-smoking and necklift

Being an ex-smoker doesn;t automatically disqualify you from having eyelid surgery or a necklift. You need to be fully assessed by a board-certified plastic surgeon.

Smoking history, or total pack years (i.e. number of packs per days times the number of years) may increase you risks of complications

Eyelid surgery generally is well-tolerated but a necklift has more risk in terms of healing problems or skin necrosis.

I agree that most Plastic Surgeons will offer blepharoplasty under a local anesthesia. It is rare that I perform this surgery under a General Anesthesia, unless the patient is having multiple procedures at the same time, or specifically requests General Anesthesia.
Wrinkled upper eyelid skin

It is tough to tell from these pictures if you would need just an upper eyelid blepharoplasty, or also a browlift. These will help to remove the excess skin. However, you will also want to do some laser resurfacing to improve the skin texture.

Just removing skin won’t help the skin that remains. laser will stimulate the production of new collagen to improve the texture.

Okay, no it is not appropriate for your plastic surgeon to both administer your propofol and also do your surgery.

This may be his normal practice. However, it is likely a violation of his accreditation. It is absolutely clear that patients are much safer having a separate anesthesia provider administer the sedation and monitor the patient while the surgeon focuses on the surgery.

Again, call AAAASF. They can be reached at 1 (888) 545-5222. It is reasonable to ask surgeons and physicians to follow the standards under which their surgery center is chartered. It may save money not to have an anesthesia provider but it is not reasonable to save money this way.
