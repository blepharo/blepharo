Blepharoplasty (or eye lift) in Portland, OR is a cosmetic procedure that can help remove excess skin to make eyelids look younger, and less “droopy”.

This procedure is usually cosmetic, but there are cases in which it is preformed for functional purposes as well.

Blepharoplasty: what is it?

A Blepharoplasty is a “lift” of the eyelids. It is a surgical procedure that will address both the upper and lower eyelids. It is mainly done to remove excess skin from the upper and the lower eyelids.

Portland, OR Upper Blepharoplasty: the incision is made in the natural crease of the eyelid and will remove the “hanging” skin that you can see on some people (as we get older the skin stretches and can cover the eyes). the incision is closed with very thin sutures that are removed in 5 days.

Lower eyelid surgery: the incision is made under the eyelashes and remove the excess skin. Absorbable sutures are used. But a lower eyelid surgery will not address wrinkled skin under the eyes, it remove excess skin.

Blepharoplasty means eyelid surgery

What is eyelid surgery blepharo

The term blepharoplasty is a fancy word for changing the shape of the eyes. Plasty is the suffix for any procedure that changes the form or shape of a body part. Blepharos is the prefix for eye in Greek. Putting the words together, you get eyelid surgery, or a surgery used to change the form or shape of the eye.

Portland Blepharoplasty is a term utilized to describe both upper eyelid as well as lower eyelid surgery. Further subdivisions are made, therefore calling a procedure and upper eyelid surgery or a lower eyelid surgery. the primary function of an upper blepharoplasty is to remove excess skin of the upper eyelid and remove excess fat in the upper eyelid compartments.

What is eyelid surgery question

The procedure normally takes 20 to 30 minutes per eye and can usually be done under local anesthesia. The primary things to keep in mind on examination are the level and position of the brows, the amount of skin to be excised, and the location of any herniated fat.

It is important not to remove too much fat thereby giving the eye a hollowed appearance. Removal of excess skin can also lead to inability to close the eyes all the way. These are both rare complications and can be prevented with a good examination and surgical technique.

What is lower eyelid surgery

Lower blepharoplasty is a much harder surgery. The surgery is used to remove excess fat, address hollows of the lower eyelid, address excess laxity of the muscle, and address excess skin of the lower eyelid. This procedure can also be done under local anesthesia, but most patients require a small amount of sedation as well. The exact procedure that needs to be done varies from patient to patient, and is dependent on the physical examination of the patient. Some patients only need excess skin redraped.

Other patients, need muscle tightening as well as removal of fat. The procedure normally takes 30 to 40 minutes per eyelid and is usually done as an outpatient procedure. It can easily be combined with other procedures of the face. The most dreaded complication is scarred tissue formation leading to scarring and drooping of the lower eyelid. This can be prevented with meticulous surgical technique as well as careful excision of skin muscle and fat. (Raghu Athre, MD, Houston Facial Plastic Surgeon)

Lower eyelid operation

Transconjuctival lower eyelid surgery refers to treating the lower eyelids through an incision hidden on the inside of the lower lid (not on the outer skin). This approach leaves no external scars and is good at addressing excess fat of the lower lids.

However if you have excess skin of your lower lids, you will need a more traditional incision which is made just under the lower eyelash line to remove the lower eyelid skin. This must be done carefully so as not to injure the orbicularis oculi muscle, which is a thin muscle that gives tone to the lower lid and helps to maintain a normal appearing eye. The type of lower eyelid surgery depends a lot on your particular anatomy. Some patients just need fat removed, which can be done via the transconjuctival approach. Some just need skin removed, and some need a combination of both skin and fat removed. Please consult with a board certified plastic surgeon regarding the rejuvenation of your lower or upper, eyelids. ( William Bruno, MD , Beverly Hills Plastic Surgeon)
