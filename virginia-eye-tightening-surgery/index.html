How is blepharoplasty performed?

Before make the incisions, the plastic surgeon marks out line. This is done so that the incisions were located along the natural contours of the eyelids.

As a result, post-surgical scars will not be noticeable. After that, the surgeon gently makes the injection of anesthetic preparations. Next, an incision is made along the planned lines.

Then, the plastic surgeon makes a small incision in the muscle layer to achieve intraorbital fat. Through this incision the surgeon excised skin and muscle from the orbital septum, which lies beneath them.

This allows the surgeon to exfoliate the muscle and skin from the orbital septum. This will open the intraorbital fat of the eyelid. When sagging intraorbital fat, the surgeon carefully performs its removal.

How long will it take to recover from eyelid surgery?

Sutures are removed already at 4-5 days after eyelid surgery in Virginia. You may be discharged from the hospital in the evening after surgery or the next day. As after any surgery, you have to be patient.

Many patients experience some discomfort, bruising and swelling. All this disappears after cold compresses and pain medication. Within 5-7 days, you will discover a decrease in bruising around the eyes.
