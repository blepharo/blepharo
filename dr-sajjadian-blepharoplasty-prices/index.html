Local Anesthesia for Facial Cosmetic Surgery

The overall facial aesthetic surgery experience should be positive in every aspect. I strongly suggest that patients who do not want to be aware of any sensation (sound, smell, the occasional “tug”, etc.) undergo sedation with either “twilight sleep” administered via an IV or general anesthesia.

Some patients prefer to avoid sedation while others desire a “wake me up when it’s over” type of experience. There is no right or wrong- just be honest with your surgeon about the type of experience you are seeking.

Eyelid surgery can be done under local anesthesia only

You’re right about the eyelid procedures being done under local anesthesia only, and I would recommend going to a board certified plastic surgeon. The procedure may take 1-2 hours depending on what you’d like done.

Also, if you have extensive upper eyelid skin that obstructs your vision you may qualify for insurance to cover this procedure. You’ll first need a visual field test that can be done by any eye doctor.

Tired eyes…

There is excess bulk on the upper lid giving a “curtain” appearance. Your lower lids also appear full and “baggy” giving a heavy look. The combination produces an appearance of tiredness as if you’ve been up all night.

An upper blepharoplasty with removal of the excess fat of the upper lid and slight removal of the skin will make a huge difference. Men should have less removal of skin, but the usual removal of fat so as to not appear overly feminized.

The lower lid excess can be addressed with a trans conjunctival (incision inside the lower lid) fat removal. You may or may not require lower lid laser to make the skin smoother on the lower lids, but it is difficult to assess in your photos.

Fat repositioning with lower blepharoplasty

Different surgeons have different techniques which work best for them. You are likely to see a big improvement in your appearance with fat removal with or without fat repositioning. I prefer not to reposition fat at the time of surgery

I remove fat and then fill any hollow areas with Restylane several weeks later. I find better, more predictable results with this method, but this is my own preference and other surgeons will prefer a different technique

My advice would be to get 2-3 opinions and then go with whichever surgeon you feel the most comfortable with.

Type of Crease in Upper Blepharoplasty?

First you should be evaluated for forehead ptosis. The forehead becomes droopy with age and contribute to upper eyelid fullness. Sometimes, endoscopic forehead lift will resolve the problem without any eyelid surgery and occasionally, both procedures need to be done.

Dr. Ali Sajjadian Lower Lid Blepharoplasty Procedure Photos

In the latter case, much less skin is removed and a more natural result is obtained. Too many blepharoplasties are done when the actual problem is forehead ptosis.

Lower lid scars

Lower lid scars can heal extremely well. If you require skin excision the incision is usually jsut below the lash line. If you just need fat removal, it is done along the conjunctiva internally.

Dark Lower Lids Not Bruising

One of the most difficult problems to deal with in eyelid surgery is dark lower lids. The main reason for the dark circles is the appearance of blood vessels close to the skin with associated thin skin

Light reflection also has a part to play. And a complaint that we all often hear is that the dark circles are worse after surgery. I believe that this has to due with a reduction of pressure on the vessels thus allowing them to engorge more and therefore creating more darkness

Upper Eyelid Plastic Surgery By Dr. Ali Sajjadian

I and everyone else really haven’t figured out the answer yet. Makeup seems to be the only solution. I’d say a tan but this is so bad for the skin leading to advanced aging.

Swelling after eyelid surgery can persist for a long time

After eyelid surgery (blepharoplasty) swelling is very common. I tell my patients that most of the swelling will go away in 2-3 weeks, but minor swelling can persist for months. Also, there will probably be a difference in the healing between the right and the left

So, what you are describing and what I see on the photos is not unusual and may last for several more weeks. I advise my patients to limit salt intake because this can make swelling longer than normal
Don’t be concerned just yet and make sure to follow your surgeon’s recommendations.
