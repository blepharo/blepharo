Keloid scar formation in aesthetic surgery

Keloids can form in any area of tissue injury-whether from surgery, trauma or burns. Keloids are firm and rubbery lesions or shiny hard nodules. They are caused by overgrowth of the collagen in scar tissue

A keloid scar can be itchy and painful. The best predictor of keloid formation is a previous keloid. Certainly, darker skinned individuals are at increased risk. Keloids are most frequently seen in the sternal area, upper back and upper arms, but are also common on earlobes

The least common place to see keloid formation is the eyelid where the skin is very thin and not under any tension. This area is considered immune to thickened scars unless the incision extends too close to the nose or onto the outside cheek
