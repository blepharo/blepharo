Eye shape after blepharoplasty

Most people consider their eyelid shape to include mostly the area between their upper and lower eyelid margin, we call this the palpebral fissure. Upper and lower blepharoplasty are more designed at addressing the upper and lower eyelids above and below the lid margins

Certain techniques during blepharoplasty can be utilized to purposefully change the shape if it is needed for either functional or cosmetic reasons. For example, during lower blepharoplasty it is common to address a lax lower lid with a lateral canthopexy or canthoplasty

As I mentioned, these techniques are purposeful, planned, and are only used when needed. Your best bet is to communicate exactly what your goals are with your surgeon, that way you two can align your expectations and goals and create a treatment plan that is perfect for you, whether this includes surgery, fillers or both. Be sure to choose a Dermatologic surgeon, plastic surgeon, facial plastic surgeon, or oculoplastic surgeon who is board certified in one of these ‘core four’ cosmetic specialties.

Fat removal or repositioning

Either fat removal or repositioning of fat or a combination of the two will give you a much improved look. Every surgeon has their own technique and all can lead to a good outcome if done properly
