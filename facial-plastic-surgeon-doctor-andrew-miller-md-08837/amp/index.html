Alar base reduction only for flared nostrils?
Alarplasty without rhinoplasty...
Jul 9, 2020
The short answer is yes, you can just do alarplasty to improve the flaring of the nostrils. It is important to have a good exam, though, because if the tip is really lacking in support, then the result will not be as good when you do things like smile. In that case, it would be best to do it all together. If the tip has good support, then an alarplasty under local anethesia is fine.
Will filler fix crooked nose, or should I consider a revision?
Filler after revision...
Jul 9, 2020
Having a filler placed can be an excellent way to improve asymmetries not corrected by rhinoplasty. Start with a small amount of filler that can dissolve like Juvederm. If you like the way it looks, then Silicone can be injected to give you a permanent result. Be sure that your injector is very experienced at doing this.

Is it possible to get polly beak deformity and nose thinning from a rhinoplasty?
Polly beak deformity...
Jun 14, 2020
Polly beak deformity most commonly occurs in a nose with poor tip support and projection, so yes, rhinoplasty can cause this. An experienced rhinoplasty surgeon is careful to avoid this situation however. When the patient has thicked skin, it can be harder to get the projection needed. The best thing to do is to consult with a few surgeons that can provide computer imaging of what they can do.
