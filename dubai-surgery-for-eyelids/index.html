Indications for eyelid surgery:

– The overhang of the skin the upper eyelids
– “Bags”, fat herniation under the eyes.

Contraindications to the eyelid surgery:

– Disorders of blood clotting
– Chronic diseases
– Oncological diseases
– All types of Diabetes
– Disorders of the thyroid gland
– Diseases of the cardiovascular system

Types of blepharoplasty.

The operation is performed mostly under general anesthesia. Mostly it takes half an hour to an hour, rarely longer, and is divided into the following types:

  1. The upper blepharoplasty (upper eyelid surgery).
  2. Lower blepharoplasty (surgery on the lower eyelid). It can be carried out both on the outside of the lid, and the inner so-called transconjunctival eyelid surgery.

Surgical technique

Operation consists in the following – the doctor makes the marking of the operative field, and then administered anesthetic medications to the patient.

After that, the doctor makes a small incision, has access to the intraorbital fat, cuts off the skin with the muscles from the orbital septum.

Then he or removes excess fat, or carries excision of excess skin on the eyelids. After this operation is completed, wounds are closed with stitches. Since the surgeon makes an incision as possible along the folds or wrinkles, seams are invisible.

Recovery after blepharoplasty

After surgery on eyelids there is some swelling in the eye area, which takes place within a few weeks, making it possible to evaluate the results of the operation.

The patient is able to leave the hospital the next day, and return to normal life for the fourth day.

In this case you must observe certain restrictions:

  1. Do not strain your eyes.
  2. Does not do the work during one week.
  3. Wear sunglasses.
  4. Swimming pool, sauna and solarium just after doctor’s permission.

Unfortunately, no surgery can not be without a certain proportion of the risk of complications and adverse effects. Eyelid surgery is no exception to this. Blepharoplasty involves the removal of upper and lower eyelids excess skin, excess fat and muscle.

Severe complications and problems associated with blepharoplasty are rare. As a general rule, cosmetic eyelid surgery has potentially less risk of complications than other more invasive cosmetic surgeries. Patients who have decided to undergo plastic surgery on eyes should be aware that any surgery has a risk of infection, bleeding, a large bruises and negative reactions to anesthesia.

Potential complications and adverse effects which are possible after blepharoplasty:

  1. Insufficient removal of skin on the eyelids
  2. The removal of excess skin on the upper and lower eyelids
  3. Ectropion
  4. Formation of cysts as a reaction to the suture material (very rarely)
  5. a bad (not cosmetic) scar on the eyelids
  6. Long-existing edema (often when is combined with Dysport or Botox before eyelid surgery)
  7. Inflammation of the eyelids (conjunctivitis)
  8. indrawing in the corners of the eyes (when applying a special inner seam – goes through 2-3 months).
  9. the change the shape of the eyelids after blepharoplasty is not a complication, but the result of the eye lid lift, as the surgeon lift the eyelid tissue and eyes a little change shape.
  10. the asymmetry of the eyelids after blepharoplasty at the first 1-2 weeks is possible due to different swelling and bruising.
  11. Increased lacrimation usually happens in the first 2-3 days after cosmetic surgery under eyes.

In general, side effects after blepharoplasty are short-term and severe complications are very rare.
