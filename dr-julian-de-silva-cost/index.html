Blepharoplasty cost varies based on geography and competition

There are several reasons that blepharoplasty and plastic surgical fees vary. Sometimes geographical location and large urban areas have higher costs, and these costs are reflected in the fees charged to patients. Sometimes better known doctors want to charge to be on the upper tier of fees

The best option in my opinion is to find a reputable surgeon who charges moderate fees and has a reputation for excellent results. Last year we treated patiets from 10 states and in many cases it was from a referral of a satisfied patient, in part due to the reasonable fees and good results

Hard lump after blepharoplasty is likely a granuloma or suture

Although a cyst is certainly a possibility causing a lower eyelid hard lump after blepharoplasty, the fact that this lump appeared so soon after surgery makes a granuloma or foreign body reaction more likely.

If sutures were placed beneath the lower eyelid during surgery, the suture may have caused a form of scar tissue called a granuloma to occur. I agree with massage as the first thing to do. However if the hard lump is still present after 6 months then surgical exploration may be necessary.

I also see lid retraction or a pulling down of the eyelid which is likely due to the external incision doen during blepharoplasty

Lower eyelids

There are generally 3 ways to address hollowness of the lower eyelids. First is to have surgery (usually under IV sedation). Second is to have fillers which usually lasts about 6 months. Third is to have a fat transfer which can last for several years but may need to be repeated after 4-6 months in order to get the correct amount of fat to survive.

Delivery of propofol

You raise an excellent and very controversial topic, both amongst physicians as well as dentists. Propofol is a rapidly on/off intravenous anesthetic used to induce or maintain a level of anesthesia to allow for a procedure.

Dr Julian De Silva Eyelid Surgery What To Expect Results

However, because of its rapid properties its use requires a great deal of training by the doctor and staff. All involved need to fully understand each phase of the anesthetic and monitor for even the subtle changes that precede an untoward event, and if untoward event occurs, all need to be trained to respond.

This level of training requires continual practice and preparation. The American Society of Plastic Surgeons and each state have guidelines for certifying centers that are capable of delivering this level of anesthesia. Having said all that, the bottom line is, I am a surgeon and want to remain focused on what each patient expects from my surgery – a favorable outcome provided in a safe environment.

For me, that means working with an anesthesiologist to deliver an individualized anesthetic for the specific procedure being performed.

Whether to do Upper or Lower Blepharoplasty

I, as a physician, should not advise you whether to do an upper or lower blepharoplasty when “BOTH are really bad.” Our job is to advise you what can be done and what the results are most likely to be and what the risks and possible complications are

Only you can decide what to do. You should do whatever bothers you the most and fits your budget and your risk tolerance. You, of course, also need to understand that after surgery the upper and lower lids will look significantly out of sync if you do only one
