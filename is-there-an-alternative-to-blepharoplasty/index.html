Is There An Alternative To Blepharoplasty?

A reasonable alternative to upper eyelid surgery is Ultherapy. Ultherapy is the only FDA approved device for non-surgical facelifting.

Small surgery goes a long way

The upper and lower lids of the aging face respond very differently to treatment options. Typically, the droopy upper lid responds very well to blepharoplasty. This is a very small operation that can be done under local anesthesia and can have profound effects. Unfortunately non surgical options do not work very well in the upper lid. The lower lid is a different story. The tear trough responds very well to facial fillers.

A possible alternative to lower eyelid blepharoplasty

Although a surgical procedure is the most effective way to treat upper eyelid hooding, a surrogate, less invasive procedure can be performed. In many cases, eyelid hooding is worsened by a progressive drop in the position of the eyebrow. This is most dramatic on the lateral aspect (towards the ear) aspect. Therefore, a “chemical browlift” could help.

This is done by injecting small amounts of a neuromodulator like Botox or Dysport UNDER the brow. The product effectively weakens the portion of muscle responsible for pulling down on the brow. If this is weakened, it allows for the forehead muscle to pull up on the brow and may make the eyelids look better at the same time. This effect is variable in different patients, but may be an option for you if you want to avoid lower eyelid blepharoplasty. ( Michael M. Kim, MD , Portland Facial Plastic Surgeon)

Minimally invasive options to rejuvenate the eyes and forehead

The effects of age, sun, genetics and facial expression have additive effects in and around the eyes. The most common complaint I receive on consultation are a tired or sad appearance. The “bags” under the eyes are secondary to pseudoherniated fat and a decent of the midfacial soft tissues.

To correct these a variety of soft tissue fillers and neuromodulatos such as Dysport or Botox can be used to accentuate the correct areas and cause elevation of the brows. Botox & Juvederm May Help Correct Sagging Eyelids

The area around the eyes is usually the first area to show the effects of facial aging. This not only makes us look older, but also makes us look tired.

Many patients would like to address this problem but aren’t quite ready for surgery. Non surgical alternatives are very attractive to this group of people.

It’s important to realize that these modalities of treatment are temporizing maneuvers and that for most people blepharoplasty surgery will ultimately be necessary to fix their problem.

Even when we set the clock back, it continues to tick. There are several categories of non surgical treatment of the peri-orbital region.

These include life style modifications, the use of injectable fillers, botox injections and skin re-surfacing. Each of these approaches has limited value in improving the appearance of the peri-orbital region.

Life style changes can be somewhat helpful. Examples might include lowering salt intake, getting more sleep, optimizing treatment of allergies and maintaining proper hydration.

In some cases the strategic use of botox can create mild brow elevation that may also impact the eyelid. Botox can also decrease fine wrinkles of the lower eyelids and crowsfeet at the corners of the eyes. Occasionally injectable fillers such as juvederm and restylene can be used to fill in tear trough deformities to minimize the impact of lower lid fat accumulations. Skin resurfacing with chemical peels and lasers can help to minimize fine wrinkles that occur around the eyes.

All of these treatments can help buy time, but when peri-orbital aging is severe more aggressive maneuvers are necessary. Under these circumstances blepharoplasty surgery and browlift surgery may need to be considered.

Every patient’s situation is unique and therefore treatment needs to be individualized. If your considering treatment of your eyelids make sure you consult a board certified plastic surgeon. (Richard J. Bruneteau, MD, Omaha Plastic Surgeon)
