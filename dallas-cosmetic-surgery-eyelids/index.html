Indications and contraindications

Indications for blepharoplasty (eyelid surgery).

  1. Excess skin of the upper and lower eyelids (the overhang);
  2. Bulging of the upper and lower eyelid fatty tissue (fat “bags” of eyelid);
  3. The wrinkles in the lower eyelid area;
  4. Omitted outer corners of the eyes;
  5. Deep wrinkles in the lower eyelids and around the eyes;
  6. The desire to change the shape of the eyes.

Contraindications for blepharoplasty.

  • High blood pressure and intraocular pressure.
  • Dry eye.
  • Cataract.
  • Thyroid disease.
