North Carolina Under Eye Plastic Surgery Price

Blepharoplasty (from Greek Blepharon – Eyelid) in North Carolina is a popular plastic surgery, which aimed at improve the appearance, rejuvenation and removal of a number of cosmetic imperfections. Most often, blepharoplasty performed after 35 – 40 years old when develops age deformation eyelids, especially the upper eyelid.

Blepharoplasty can eliminate overhang of the upper eyelid (the so-called “tired” look), “bags”, deep wrinkles. This operation is also used to change the eye shape (drooping of the outer corner of the eye, the Asian eye shape, etc.).

Before performing eyelid surgery, you should undergo a thorough medical examination to rule out contraindications to baggy eyelid surgery. This operation does not affect visual acuity as the structure of the eyeball (the cornea or lens) is not affected during its execution.

Types of blepharoplasty in North Carolina

1. Upper eyelid blepharoplasty.

With this operation can eliminate the excess skin and fatty tissue in the area of the upper eyelid. In some cases, simultaneously with the removal of excess tissue and fat hernia is performed lifting circular muscles of the eye. The incision is made at the level of the upper eyelid crease in the depth of the eye sockets.

2. The lower eyelid blepharoplasty.

With this operation can remove “bags” under the eyes and a hernia. There are several options of the lower eyelid surgery in North Carolina, depending on the operative access. When transcutaneous lower lid blepharoplasty in North Carolina, incision passes to 1 mm inside the lash line.

An alternative to the above method is transconjunctival blepharoplasty, when the access to the tissue of the lower eyelid is carried out in the mucosal area. The advantage of said method is the lack of visually noticeable sutures after eye lid operation.

3. Circular blepharoplasty.

The operation involves the simultaneous correction of the upper and lower eyelids.

4. Correction of the eye shape.

Most often with the help of this operation can correct the Asian eye shape. During the operation, the surgeon removes epicanthus and forms the crease of the upper eyelid, which characteristic for Caucasoid eye shape.

Technique of blepharoplasty.

Cosmetic surgery for eyes is performed on an outpatient basis after admission to the hospital. Usually local anesthesia is used, at the request of the patient’s, intervention can be performed under local anesthesia.

Incisions are made in the natural crease of the upper eyelid, along the lash line and the inner surface of the mucous membrane of the lower eyelid (transconjunctival) to achieve better results.

The duration of the blepharoplasty treatment depends on the volume of interference and presence associated technical difficulties. On average, blepharoplasty in NC can last from 40 minutes to 1-2 hours.

Indications for blepharoplasty.

Eyelid surgery is used to correct the following cosmetic defects:

  1. overhang skin of the upper eyelid with the formation of “hard” or “tired” look;
  2. excess skin or fat under the eyes (“bags”);
  3. deep wrinkles in the eye area;
  4. omitted corners of the eyes;
  5. congenital or acquired defects of the upper and lower eyelids.

With the help of blepharoplasty you can change the shape of the eyes.

A pressure bandage is applied after the cosmetic surgery for eyes, it reduces the severity of edema and bruises that occur in the early postoperative period (1-2 weeks after surgery). Immediately after surgery, may also appear excessive tearing, increased sensitivity and discomfort in the eyes.

Should refrain from eye strain (reading, watching TV) for 2 days after surgery. Physical exertion should be avoided for 3 weeks after surgery. Contact lenses can be used no earlier than 2 weeks after eyelid surgery.

The final results of operations can evaluate not earlier than 1.5 – 2 months. Provided that the patient will comply with all recommendations of the physician, the results of a blepharoplasty in NC persist up to 10 years. Thereafter, the eye lid plastic surgery can be performed again, when the patient’s wish.

All risks associated with the performance of eyelid surgery can be divided into several groups: the complications associated with anesthesia and the complications caused by surgical intervention.

Contraindications for blepharoplasty:

  • dry eye syndrome;
  • increased intraocular pressure;
  • arterial hypertension;
  • diabetes;
  • problems with blood clotting;
  • malignant neoplasms;
  • diseases of the cardiovascular system.
