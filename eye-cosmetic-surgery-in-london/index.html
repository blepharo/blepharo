Eye Cosmetic Surgery In London

Blepharoplasty in London is one of the most popular and sought-after achievements of plastic surgery. It can be used to remove age-related changes, and congenital features upper and lower eyelids.

Blepharoplasty (from Greek blepharon – eyelid) is an operation aimed at reshaping the eyelids, the eye shape, removing excess skin and fat herniation.

When forming a new shape of eyes (“westernize” performed on Asian people), the operation also involves the removal of excess fat in the periorbital region and the formation of the skin fold in the upper eyelid.

Blepharoplasty is also effective if you want to change your appearance, for creating almond-shaped eyes, called “cat’s eyes”.

In this case, through an incision on the upper eyelid is tightened tendon and fixed to the outer corner of the eye.

Excess skin and fat are removed through incisions in the crease eyelid. Eyelid surgery is performed in a hospital under general anesthesia or local anesthesia.

Duration of operation is from 20 minutes to 1 hour. The recovery period does not exceed 14 days. The final result of surgery may be assessed within 1.5 – 2 months, when the postoperative scar go down.

The postoperative period

The postoperative period is fairly calm.  There are no pains, blurred vision and increased lacrimation. However, surgery can cause transient irritation of mucous and sensation of dryness.

Do not abuse the TV watching or work at a computer.

The stitches are removed one or two days after surgery. In their place are superimposed microwell strips is a strip of adhesive material that securely holds edges of the surgical incisions.

The strips are removed on the seventh day after blepharoplasty. Post-operative swelling disappears within a week, and postoperative scars become imperceptible after two to four weeks.

Types of eyelid surgery

Blepharoplasty of upper eyelids is a procedure aimed at the removal of the upper eyelid hernias and removing excess skin, allowing achieve the effect of “open eyes”. This type of surgery is performed through a skin incision located in the crease of the upper eyelid.

Blepharoplasty of lower eyelids helps to get rid of bags under the eyes, swelling and herniation. The seam passes under the lash line. Transconjunctival blepharoplasty in London can be performed on the lower eyelid (incision is on the inside of the eyelid). This type surgery is possible to perform, if required only redistribution or removal of adipose tissue in the area of the lower eyelid without removing excess skin.

Circular blepharoplasty is the simultaneous surgery of the upper and lower eyelids.

Transconjunctival blepharoplasty is a method that is applied to the lower eyelids and is the most sparing way of correction, because the external incision is not using, in contrast to the classical surgery.

The procedure is performed to remove subcutaneous fat hernia under the eyes, as well as the removal of excess subcutaneous fat so-called bags. It allows to smooth too pronounced furrows, make the area of the lower eyelids a more “accurate” and an open view.

This surgical approach is the least traumatic. It does not involve dissection of the subcutaneous layers and removing excess skin. Therefore, such a way of surgical intervention are no risks of disturbances of facial expressions or changes in shape of the eye.

In addition, the operation does not leave scars on her face, since the incision is performed from the inside of the lower eyelid and the outer seams are not required. Transconjunctival blepharoplasty is also called the “seamless.”

Manipulation, the essence of which consists in the removal of excess adipose tissue in the lower eyelid, lasts about 40 minutes and is generally performed under local anesthesia. The procedure is well tolerated and does not require hospitalization: The patient is generally leaves the hospital in day of the operation.

Indications and contraindications for eyelid surgery.

Indications:

  • the overhang of the skin of the upper and lower eyelids;
  • “the fat bags”;

  • the omission the lower corners of the eyes;
  • shape of the eye;

Contraindications:

  1. High blood pressure or intraocular pressure;
  2. Dry eye;
  3. Thyroid disease;
  4. Diseases of the cardiovascular system and other severe diseases of internal organs;
  5. Diabetes;
  6. Oncological diseases;
  7. Coagulation failure.
