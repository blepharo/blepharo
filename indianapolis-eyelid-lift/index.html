When eyelid surgery is needed and why changes occur

Blepharoplasty in Indianapolis will help refresh and restore the youthfulness of your face by reducing the severity and sagging eyelids, puffiness and bags under the eyes.

Indianapolis offers a number of clinics where you can make such an operation. But do not forget that plastic surgery on eyes is a very delicate matter and for your own sake will be better if you consult a professional surgeons.

Before we talk about the principle of blepharoplasty, let’s look at the structure of eyelids. Like the rest parts of the face, eyelids are composed of layers of skin, muscle and fat.

Over time, age and environmental factors such as exposure to sunlight leads to the fact that the skin support system is destroyed. As a result, the skin loses its structure and elasticity and becomes flabby.

Muscular layer weakens, and loss of skin elasticity and the action of gravity causes to sagging of eyelids. Under normal conditions, the fatty tissue gives elasticity to eyelids.

But it is depleted with time due to weakened, sag muscles, which leads to swelling and bags under the eyes.

Although this plastic surgery for eyes is effective in solving these problems, it can not help in removing wrinkles around the eyes, dark circles under the eyes, or drop of the outer edge of the eyebrow.

In such cases, eyelid surgery in Indianapolis performed in conjunction with other procedures (eg, brow lift, lipofilling), can give wonderful results.

Different people are interested in different the eyelid surgeries. Some people can be interested in lower eyelid surgery, or the so-called lower blepharoplasty.

This variant is the most popular, which is not surprising. After all, the bags under the eyes and swelling appear just on the lower eyelids. Although also a popular option is a blepharoplasty of the upper eyelids.

The principle of blepharoplasty

Although a surgeon may use any of several existing techniques, standard surgical technique involves the removal or redistribution of fat tissue and also the removal excess skin and muscle.

Bags and puffiness eyelids mostly are caused by protruding intraorbital fat deposits.

Under normal conditions, orbital septum fixes deposits of fat in certain areas. But over time, it partially loses its integrity and weakens, and fat deposits come forward, creating bags and puffiness.

Outer blepharoplasty consists in removing and forming the excess intraorbital fat through an incision on the outside of the lower eyelid.

The incision passes through the the bottom edge of the eyelashes, indented 1mm.

This makes it invisible, like incision with otoplasty. Unlike the transconjunctival blepharoplasty, to lifting the eyelids are removed and excess skin.

Transconjunctival blepharoplasty involves the removal of excess intraorbital fat through an incision in the conjunctiva (the mucous membrane on the inside surface of the eyelid).

This version of blepharoplasty is most often used in patients with a low degree of sagging skin. Transconjunctival approach avoids external scars. If you are interested in quality blepharoplasty, the price for it will depend on the skill of the plastic surgeon’s, clinic and a number of factors.

Preparation for surgery and technique of blepharoplasty

Eyelid surgery in Indianapolis IN may last from 30 minutes to an hour, depending on the intensity of intervention. The area on which the cosmetic surgery under eyes will be carried out is disinfected. Next, the anesthetist uses anesthesia.

Eyelid Surgery Procedure Indianapolis By Dr. Janet Turkle

Eyelid Surgery, is likely to be performed under local anesthesia with a sedative when the eyelids and the area around them, anesthetized, and you are in a relaxed state. An alternative to this option may be general anesthesia, in which you will sleep.

Before making the incisions, the plastic surgeon mark up lines on which they will pass. This will help position them along the natural contours of the eyelid, so the scars will eventually be invisible. After that the doctor carefully injects anesthetics.

The incision carefully performed along the planned lines. The surgeon makes a small incision in the muscle layer to reach the intraorbital fat.

The surgeon excises the skin and muscle from the orbital septum through this incision. This allows the doctor to pull the skin and muscle from the orbital septum and to open intraorbital fat eyelid.

In cases of sagging intraorbital fat, the doctor gently removes it, while minimizing any bleeding. If you have excess skin on the eyelids, they will be removed.

After blepharoplasty, on skin edges are superimposed the thinnest stitches and sterile patch, as well as in breast augmentation.
